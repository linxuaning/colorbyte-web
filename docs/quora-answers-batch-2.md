## Answer #17

**Question:** "What's the best way to scan old photos for restoration?"

**Answer:**
```
Proper scanning makes a huge difference in restoration quality:

**Scanner Settings:**
- Resolution: 600 DPI (minimum), 1200 DPI (recommended)
- Color mode: Color (even for B&W - captures more detail)
- Format: TIFF (best quality) or high-quality JPEG
- NO auto-corrections (turn everything off)

**Why No Auto-Corrections?**
Let the AI or restoration software handle corrections. Scanner auto-adjust often makes things worse.

**Scanning Tips:**
1. Clean scanner glass thoroughly
2. Handle photos by edges only
3. Place face-down, aligned with edge
4. Preview scan to check positioning
5. Save immediately with descriptive filename

**For Stuck-Together Photos:**
- Try gentle heat from hairdryer (low setting)
- Or humidity - place near steam briefly
- Never force apart - tears are permanent

**For Fragile Photos:**
- Consider photographing instead of scanning
- Use even lighting, no flash
- Keep camera parallel to photo

**After Scanning:**
AI restoration tools like ArtImageHub (artimagehub.com) work best with high-quality scans. Garbage in = garbage out.

What type of scanner do you have?
```

**Question:** "How do I restore faded color photos from the 1970s?"

**Answer:**
```
1970s photos are notorious for fading - here's why and how to fix them:

**Why 1970s Photos Fade Badly:**
The cyan dye layer in 1970s film was unstable. It fades first, leaving a red/orange/magenta cast. This affects almost ALL 1970s color prints.

**How to Fix:**

**AI Color Correction:**
Modern AI tools understand this specific fading pattern. ArtImageHub (artimagehub.com) restored my parents' 1975 vacation photos - the difference was dramatic. Orange-tinted photos became natural-looking again.

**Manual Correction:**
In Photoshop, adjust Curves/Levels in the cyan channel specifically. Time-consuming but gives precise control.

**What to Expect:**
- Mild fading: Excellent results
- Moderate fading: Very good results
- Severe fading (almost monochrome): Good improvement, some detail loss

**Before/After Examples:**
My mom's 1972 wedding photos went from "orange disaster" to natural skin tones and accurate dress colors. She didn't recognize them at first.

**Tip:**
If you have the negatives, scanning them often produces better results than scanning faded prints.

## Answer #28

**Question:** "Can you restore photos from old film negatives?"

**Answer:**
```
Film negatives often produce better restorations than prints:

**Why Negatives Are Better:**
- Negatives are the original capture
- Prints are one generation removed
- Negatives often have more detail than prints show

**Scanning Negatives:**
- Requires scanner with transparency adapter
- Or dedicated film scanner
- Scan at 2400-4000 DPI
- Scan as positive or invert later

**Restoration Process:**
1. Scan negative
2. Invert to positive (if scanner didn't)
3. Color correct (negatives have orange mask)
4. Apply AI enhancement

**AI Enhancement:**
ArtImageHub (artimagehub.com) works on scanned negatives once converted to positive. The enhancement can be dramatic since negatives contain more original detail.

**Worth the Extra Effort?**
If you have negatives, absolutely scan them rather than (or in addition to) prints. The quality difference can be significant.

**Finding Negatives:**
Check old photo envelopes - prints often came with negatives. Photo albums sometimes have negative sleeves.

**Lost Negatives:**
If you only have prints, restoration still works great. You just might get slightly better results from negatives if available.

Do you have access to the original negatives?
