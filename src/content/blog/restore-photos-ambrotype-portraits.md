# Preserving Victorian Ambrotype Portraits

Ambrotypes are glass-plate photographs that appear as positives when backed with dark material — among the most technically delicate photographs in family collections. These mid-19th century portraits require specialized handling and restoration approaches.

## The Challenge

Ambrotype damage often involves both the photographic emulsion and the glass substrate — the glass can crack or shatter, while the collodion emulsion separates from the glass in sheets. Many ambrotypes show partial emulsion loss that requires reconstruction.

## How AI Helps

AI restoration trained on glass-plate photography handles ambrotype emulsion loss through inpainting algorithms that reconstruct missing image areas from surrounding context. The algorithm respects the distinctive tonal characteristics of ambrotype photography.
