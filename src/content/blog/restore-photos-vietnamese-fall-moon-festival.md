The Vietnamese Mid-Autumn Festival (Tết Trung Thu) is one of the most beloved children's celebrations in Vietnamese culture, featuring lantern processions, mooncakes, and the gathering of family to watch the full harvest moon. Vietnamese American family photographs from this celebration document the maintenance of this beloved tradition in American contexts, often adapted to the specific conditions of American neighborhoods while maintaining the essential elements of the celebration.

## Cultural Heritage

Mid-Autumn Festival photographs document the specific visual elements of this celebration: the diverse lanterns that children carry in evening processions (from traditional star-shaped paper lanterns to elaborate battery-powered designs), the mooncakes prepared or purchased for the occasion, the community gatherings organized by Vietnamese American associations, and the family moments of celebration that connect American-born children to Vietnamese cultural traditions. For Vietnamese American families, these photographs document cultural transmission across generations.

## Festival Documentation

Mid-Autumn Festival photographs face preservation challenges related to their evening celebration contexts (low-light conditions make photography challenging) and to the storage conditions in Vietnamese American households. AI restoration addresses these preservation challenges, recovering the visual documentation of cultural celebrations that maintain Vietnamese heritage in American communities.
