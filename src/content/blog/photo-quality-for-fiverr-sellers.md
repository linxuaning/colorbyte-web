## Why Does Gig Image Quality Impact Fiverr Sales?

The Fiverr buyer experience starts in search results. A buyer types "logo design" or "blog post writing" and sees a grid of sixteen to twenty gig thumbnails. At this stage, they cannot read your reviews in detail, cannot see your portfolio, and cannot evaluate your description. What they can evaluate in about two seconds is your gig image.

Three things happen based on that image:

**Click-through rate.** High-quality, clear images get more clicks. Fiverr's algorithm monitors click-through rate as a relevance signal. Over time, gigs with higher CTR earn better placement, creating a compounding advantage for sellers with professional images.

**First impression of quality.** Buyers consistently project the quality of your gig image onto the quality of your work. A blurry photo of your design samples implies blurry design thinking. A grainy screenshot of your writing suggests low-effort output. This is not rational, but it is real and well-documented in marketplace research.

**Mobile optimization.** An estimated 40–60% of Fiverr browsing happens on mobile devices where small thumbnails dominate the screen. At mobile thumbnail size, low-quality images become even harder to distinguish — you need extra sharpness and clarity to read as professional.

## What Are Fiverr's Image Requirements?

Fiverr requires gig images at a minimum of 550×370 pixels in 3:2 aspect ratio (JPEG or PNG, up to 50MB). That minimum is a floor, not a target.

For professional results:
- **Upload size**: 1650×1110px (3× minimum, same ratio) — crisp on all displays
- **Format**: PNG for graphics and screenshots; high-quality JPEG for photographs
- **Content**: Show the work, not just you — mockups, samples, results

## Common Photo Quality Problems by Seller Type

### Design Sellers

**Problem: Screenshots compressed multiple times.** Designers often capture their work with screenshots (Cmd+Shift+4 / Snipping Tool), save as JPEG, then screenshot again when updating mockups. Each JPEG save adds compression artifacts that build up into visible blocking and color banding.

**Fix:** Export directly from your design software (Figma, Illustrator, Canva) to PNG. For existing screenshots with blocking, run them through the [JPEG Artifact Remover](/jpeg-artifact-remover) which uses SwinIR to clean compression artifacts before uploading to Fiverr.

### Photography Sellers

**Problem: Portfolio samples taken in mixed indoor light.** Product photographers often shoot indoors without studio lighting, resulting in grain, color casts, and soft focus that makes their work appear less polished than it actually is.

**Fix:** For original camera files you can reshoot, add a ring light or move to window light. For existing photos with grain, the [Photo Denoiser](/photo-denoiser) uses NAFNet to remove noise while preserving genuine texture. For soft focus from camera shake, the [Photo Deblurrer](/photo-deblurrer) can recover sharpness.

### Video Sellers

**Problem: Thumbnails exported from compressed video.** Video editors often grab a frame from their demo reel as a gig image. Video frames — especially from H.264 or H.265 compressed footage — contain blocking artifacts and reduced sharpness that look significantly worse as a static image.

**Fix:** Export your best frame as a PNG directly from your editing software (Premiere, DaVinci Resolve, Final Cut) rather than screenshotting the timeline. If working with existing JPEG exports, [JPEG Artifact Remover](/jpeg-artifact-remover) will clean compression artifacts before you upload.

### Writing and Content Sellers

**Problem: Generic stock or low-resolution text mockups.** Many writing sellers use free stock images or basic mockups at low resolution, resulting in gig images that look identical to hundreds of competitors.

**Fix:** Create a custom mockup showing your actual work — a screenshot of a well-formatted article, a product description on a clean background, or a styled excerpt. Export at high resolution from Canva or Figma and sharpen with the [Photo Enhancer](/photo-enhancer) before uploading.
