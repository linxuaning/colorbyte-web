## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can small passport photos be enlarged to usable size?**

Short answer: Yes, with limitations. Our [AI upscaler](/ai-photo-upscaler) can enlarge small identity photos significantly while maintaining clarity. Original photo quality determines results—professional photos enlarge better than photo booth images. Expect good improvement but not magazine quality.

**How do I restore passport photos that are still in the document?**

Short answer: Scan at high resolution without removing the photo from the document—removal risks damaging both. Use flatbed scanner carefully. Our [restoration tool](/enhance-old-photos) works with scans. If the photo must be removed, consider professional document conservation.

**Are old passport photos useful for genealogy research?**

Short answer: Very useful. They confirm identity, show physical appearance at specific dates, and often include valuable document information (dates, places, official stamps). They connect to immigration records, manifests, and naturalization files. They're particularly valuable for immigrant ancestors.

**Can I restore passport photos from countries that no longer exist?**

Short answer: Yes—these are especially valuable historically. Photos from the Austro-Hungarian Empire, Soviet bloc countries, or other former nations document specific historical periods. The restoration process is the same, and the historical significance may be greater.

**How do I find ancestors' passport photos if I don't have the documents?**

Short answer: Check with family members who may have papers. Naturalization records include photos and are searchable through Ancestry.com and USCIS historical records. Immigration museums (Ellis Island, etc.) have collections. National Archives holds many immigration photo records.
