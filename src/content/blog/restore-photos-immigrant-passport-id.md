The passport photograph is the most minimal possible portrait: face against a plain background, designed for official identification rather than aesthetics. For immigrant families, these photographs are identity documents — the official record of who someone was at a specific moment in time.

## Official Photography Conventions

Passport and immigration ID photographs have followed specific conventions since the introduction of photographic identification. These conventions — lighting, framing, expression — create photographs that look similar across decades even as the technology producing them has changed.

## Identity Documents as Family Archive

For immigrant families, official identification documents — passports, naturalization papers, work permits — are among the most carefully preserved family documents. The photographs associated with them carry the weight of the documents themselves.

## Small Format, Large Significance

Passport photographs are small — the standard size has been approximately 2x2 inches for much of the 20th century. This small size means small faces. High-resolution scanning (1200+ DPI) is essential for useful face detail in identification photographs.
