The teacher career photograph spans from the formal portrait in the faculty photograph to the informal classroom snapshot to the retirement ceremony. For families of teachers, these photographs document a career of public service.

## Classroom Documentation

Photographs taken in classrooms document the specific educational environments of different eras: the chalk boards and wooden desks of the 1940s, the audiovisual equipment of the 1960s, the computer labs of the 1990s. This environmental documentation has increasing historical value.

## Faculty Photographs

School faculty photographs are specific institutional documentation — the formal annual group portrait that places individuals in the institutional context of a specific year. These photographs connect to student memories and to the institutional history of schools that may no longer exist.

## Retirement Ceremonies

Teacher retirement ceremonies produce photographs of a milestone that concludes decades of public service. The photographs often include former students, the entire institutional memory visible in a single gathering.

## Getting the Best Results

Start with the highest-quality scan you can produce — 600 DPI minimum for standard prints, 1200 DPI for small prints or photographs with faces you want to identify. Color mode scanning, even for black-and-white photographs, gives AI restoration algorithms more information to work with.

After restoration, compare the result with the original at full zoom. Check faces carefully to ensure identity is preserved, and note any areas where AI may have filled in damaged sections with plausible but uncertain reconstructions.
