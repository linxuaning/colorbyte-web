## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can dark church interior photos be brightened without looking unnatural?**

Short answer: Yes. Our [AI enhancement tool](/enhance-old-photos) carefully lifts shadows while preserving the atmosphere of church settings. The goal is visibility while maintaining the reverent, often softly-lit character of sanctuary photography. The sacred feeling is preserved.

**How do I restore wedding photos where the bride's white dress has lost detail?**

Short answer: AI restoration can recover white dress details that have faded or become overexposed. Lace patterns, beading, and fabric texture can often be enhanced. Our tool is trained to handle the challenge of white wedding gowns against various backgrounds.

**Can faded 1970s church wedding photos be restored to original color?**

Short answer: Usually yes, dramatically. 1970s color photos commonly fade to magenta or yellow, but the underlying image information often survives. Color correction can restore much of the original vibrancy—faded bridesmaids dresses regain their colors, and the overall scene becomes recognizable again.

**Should I restore wedding photos after a divorce?**

Short answer: Consider the photos' value to children and family history. The wedding still happened and documents your life. Children may want access to images of their parents' wedding. You might restore but not display, or focus restoration on individual or family-only shots. The decision is personal.

**How do I find church wedding photos if the original prints are lost?**

Short answer: Contact the photographer if known (studios keep archives). Ask family members who may have copies. The church may have records of photographers who worked there. Check with wedding party members. Professional photography archives sometimes hold old negatives.
