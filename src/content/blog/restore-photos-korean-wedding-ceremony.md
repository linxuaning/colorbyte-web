# Restoring Korean Traditional Wedding Photographs

Korean traditional wedding photographs document one of the most visually distinctive wedding ceremonies in the Asian American photographic heritage: the Korean couple in traditional hanbok dress, the specific ceremonial elements of the paebaek ceremony where the couple bows to the groom's family, and the specific ritual foods and objects that constitute the traditional Korean wedding ritual. For Korean American families, these photographs document the maintenance of traditional marriage ceremonies within American wedding contexts.

## Cultural Heritage

Korean wedding photographs document both the traditional ceremony elements and the specific adaptations made in American contexts: the balance of traditional Korean ceremony with American wedding customs that characterizes Korean American weddings of different generations and assimilation levels. The photographs show the specific hanbok styles (which have their own historical evolution and regional variations), the ceremonial objects (the wooden geese, the wine cups, the embroidered cloths), and the specific family arrangements of the ceremony.
