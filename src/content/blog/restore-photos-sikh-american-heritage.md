# Restoring Sikh American Heritage Photographs

Sikh American family photographs document the heritage of a distinctive religious community with a specific visual culture: the dastar (turban) worn by Sikh men as a religious obligation creates an immediately recognizable visual identity, the specific white salwar kameez of the Khalsa, and the distinct setting of the gurdwara (Sikh temple) with its golden dome and the service of langar (communal free meal) that is central to Sikh practice. Photographs of Sikh American families show both religious observance and the community built around the gurdwara.

## Cultural Heritage

Sikh American photographs document the specific life of gurdwara communities: the daily prayers and services, the major Sikh religious celebrations (Gurpurab celebrations honoring the Sikh Gurus, Baisakhi), the langar service where all are welcome to share a free meal regardless of background, and the community social services that gurdwaras often provide. For Sikh American families, the gurdwara community photographs document the primary institution of religious and social life.

## Religious Community Documentation

Sikh American photographs face preservation challenges related to the diverse origins of Sikh immigration — primarily from the Punjab region of India and Pakistan — and the varied storage conditions in Sikh American households across different eras and economic circumstances.
