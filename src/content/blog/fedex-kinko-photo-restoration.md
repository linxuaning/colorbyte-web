FedEx Office (formerly Kinkos) is widely accessible — over 2,000 locations across the US — and many people associate it with document and photo printing. Here's what FedEx Office actually offers for old family photographs, and how to use it as part of a restoration and printing workflow.

---

## Does FedEx Office Restore Old Photos?

**FedEx Office does not offer photo restoration services.** It is a print and document services center — it prints what you provide, at the quality you provide it.

If you upload a faded, scratched old photo to FedEx Office's print service, it prints the faded, scratched photo. The restoration needs to happen before printing.

---

## What FedEx Office Can Print

**Standard photo prints:**
- 4×6, 5×7, 8×10, and some larger sizes at select locations

**Large format printing:**
- Poster prints up to 24×36 and larger
- Canvas prints (some locations)
- Banner prints

**Same-day availability:**
- Most FedEx Office locations offer same-day or 1-hour printing for standard sizes
- Large format prints typically same-day or next-day

**Quality:**
- FedEx Office print quality is generally better than pharmacy kiosks (Walgreens, CVS)
- Professional inkjet or laser printing with calibrated color
- Paper options at some locations (matte, glossy, fine art)

---

## Where FedEx Office Shines for Photo Projects

**Large format for memorial displays:** FedEx Office's large format printing (18×24, 24×36 poster size) is one of the most accessible options for memorial service portrait displays and photo boards. Restored portrait photographs print well at poster size from HD files.

**Same-day availability for urgent needs:** For memorial services, family reunions, or other events where the printing is needed today, FedEx Office's wide availability and same-day service is a genuine advantage.

**Canvas wraps:** Some FedEx Office locations offer canvas wraps, which work well for restored portrait photographs for home display.

**Color accuracy:** FedEx Office's print quality is generally calibrated and consistent — restored photographs print with accurate colors when the file quality is high.
