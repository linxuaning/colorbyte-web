## What Kinds of Damage Most Commonly Affect Baby Photos?

The most common damage patterns in family baby photo collections:

**Physical damage from handling** — creases from being folded, surface abrasion from being slid across surfaces, corner tears from album removal. These are the most straightforward for AI to repair because surrounding pixel data is intact.

**Chemical deterioration** — yellowing from acid migration in non-archival albums, fading from light exposure, and the brownish foxing spots caused by mold and metal particle oxidation in paper.

**Water damage** — tide-line staining from flooding or leaks, emulsion bubbling where moisture got between layers, and in severe cases complete emulsion separation leaving just the paper backing.

**Physical separation and tears** — photos stuck to album pages that tore during removal, or prints that cracked along stress lines during storage in temperature-extreme environments.

## How to Prepare a Damaged Baby Photo for Best Results

Scan at 600 dpi for standard 4 by 6 inch or 5 by 7 inch prints. For smaller wallet-size or album-format prints, scan at 1200 dpi. Save as PNG or TIFF rather than JPEG to preserve maximum tonal information before uploading.

If the photo is stuck to an album page, photograph it in place at high resolution with a camera and macro lens rather than risking further damage by removing it.
