# How to Restore Old Photos on Android (2026 Guide)

If you have old family photos — scanned and saved to your Android device, or photographed from physical prints — here's what Android's built-in tools can handle and the fastest path to professional-quality AI restoration.

---

## What Google Photos Can Do for Old Photos

Google Photos is installed on most Android devices and has AI-powered editing tools:

**Basic adjustments:**
- Light: Exposure, Contrast, Highlights, Shadows, Luminance
- Color: Saturation, Warmth, Tint
- Pop: Clarity and vignette
- One-tap "Enhance" (lightning bolt) — automatic adjustment

**Magic Eraser (Pixel phones and Google One subscribers):** Removes objects from photos using AI fill. For isolated scratches against simple backgrounds, this can work. Results vary on old photos with complex backgrounds.

**Photo Unblur (Pixel 6+):** AI sharpening for blurry photos. Works on motion blur in relatively modern photos; less effective on the type of face softening caused by photographic paper aging over decades.

**What Google Photos cannot do:** Systematic historical face reconstruction (CodeFormer-class), systematic fading correction (GFPGAN-class), or scratch pattern removal trained on historical photographic damage.

---

## Best Android Apps for Old Photo Restoration

**Snapseed (Android, free — Google):**
- Healing tool: manual scratch and blemish removal
- Portrait tool: face enhancement (modern portrait focus)
- Tune Image: comprehensive manual adjustments
- Good for moderate damage on photos that mainly need color correction
- Requires manual work; not automated

**Remini (Android, freemium):**
- AI face enhancement for old and low-quality photos
- Good result quality; subscription model ($4.99–$9.99/month)
- Best for portrait restoration; less effective for damage removal

**TouchRetouch (Android, $1.99):**
- Best-in-class scratch and object removal
- Manual selection + AI fill
- Excellent for isolated scratches and spots

## Scanning Physical Prints with Android

**Google PhotoScan (Android):** Best option for reflective prints. Takes 4 shots at different angles, eliminates glare, stitches composite. Free, Play Store.

**Microsoft Lens (Android):** Good edge detection and perspective correction. Works well for flat, matte prints.

**Stock camera:** Works for matte prints in even, indirect lighting. Position camera directly above the print, parallel, fill the frame. Avoid flash.

**Scan quality matters for restoration:** Higher resolution scan = more information for AI to work with. If the original print is very small (wallet-sized, less than 3×4 inches), scan at the highest resolution your setup supports.

## Android vs iPhone for Old Photo Restoration

Both platforms support ArtImageHub in browser — the restoration quality is identical (it's server-side). Platform differences are in the scanning apps:

**Google PhotoScan** is available for Android and iOS. On Pixel phones, additional AI tools (Magic Eraser, Photo Unblur) are available. Neither platform has a native restoration model equivalent to CodeFormer.
