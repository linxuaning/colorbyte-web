Color correction is the foundational technique of photo restoration — recovering the accurate colors that photographic deterioration has shifted, faded, or lost. Understanding how color correction works helps you set realistic expectations for what restoration can and cannot achieve.

## The Challenge

Color deterioration in photographs follows predictable patterns based on the photographic technology used. Knowing whether a photo is a Kodacolor print from 1975 or a Fujicolor print from 1985 allows targeted correction of the specific dye failure pattern for that technology.

## How AI Helps

AI color correction applies photographic technology identification as its first step — matching the image to known deterioration profiles for specific films and printing processes. This targeted approach produces more accurate results than generic color enhancement algorithms.
