# Preserving Chinese Railroad Worker Heritage Photos

Chinese railroad worker heritage photos document the labor of the 20,000 Chinese workers who built the western half of the transcontinental railroad. Despite building national infrastructure at tremendous personal cost, these workers were largely excluded from the official photographic record.

## The Challenge

The few surviving images related to Chinese railroad workers are among the most historically significant photographs in American history. Many are formal survey photos that incidentally include Chinese workers, requiring careful examination to identify and honor their presence.

## How AI Helps

AI enhancement trained on 19th-century photographic technologies recovers detail from survey and construction photographs, revealing the faces and work of Chinese railroad workers whose historical contributions were systematically erased from the official record.

## Next Steps

Gather your photographs, scan at the highest resolution available (600 DPI minimum), and visit [PhotoFix](/old-photo-restoration) to start the restoration process. AI tools can handle damage that once required expensive professional services, making preservation accessible for every family.
