# ArtImageHub vs Topaz Gigapixel AI: Old Photo Restoration Compared

Topaz Gigapixel AI is a professional-grade upscaling tool with a strong reputation among photographers and retouchers. It's known for extraordinary image upscaling — turning low-resolution images into large, print-ready files.

ArtImageHub is built specifically for old photo restoration — damage repair, face enhancement, fading correction.

These tools have different primary functions. Here's how they compare when the goal is restoring old family photos.

---

## What Each Tool Does

**Topaz Gigapixel AI** is primarily an AI upscaler. It takes a low-resolution image and increases its size — up to 6x — while reconstructing believable detail using AI. It also has some photo enhancement and denoising capability through the Topaz Photo AI suite.

Photo restoration is not its primary function. Physical damage like scratches, tears, and water stains is not what Gigapixel was built for.

**ArtImageHub** combines:
- Physical damage repair (scratches, stains, tears)
- Face restoration (CodeFormer — purpose-built for degraded face recovery)
- Image-wide enhancement (GFPGAN)
- Upscaling (Real-ESRGAN, integrated into the same pipeline)

All in one step, delivered as a web tool requiring no software installation.

---

## Pricing

**Topaz Gigapixel AI / Topaz Photo AI:**
- Topaz Photo AI (which includes Gigapixel functionality): **$199 one-time** or **$99/year** subscription
- Requires desktop software installation (Windows/Mac)
- Processing happens locally on your computer (no cloud upload)
- Significant system requirements — benefits from a dedicated GPU

**ArtImageHub:**
- **$4.99 one-time**
- Web-based — no installation required
- Works on any device with a browser
- Cloud processing — no GPU required on your end

The price difference is substantial: $199 vs $4.99. Topaz is priced for professional photographers who need it in a regular workflow. For a one-time family photo restoration project, the cost difference is hard to justify.

## Where Topaz Wins

**Upscaling quality and size.** Topaz Gigapixel AI is the industry benchmark for AI upscaling. If you need to take a 300px portrait and print it at 20"×30", Topaz produces better results at extreme scales than any web tool.

**Batch processing.** If you're processing hundreds of images, Topaz runs locally and can process batches overnight. ArtImageHub is a one-at-a-time web workflow.

**Privacy.** Photos never leave your computer with Topaz. For users with sensitive images, local processing is an advantage.

---

## Where ArtImageHub Wins

**Physical damage repair.** Topaz doesn't fix scratches, tears, water stains, or chemical degradation. If the photo is physically damaged, Topaz will upscale the damage alongside the photo. ArtImageHub addresses the damage before upscaling.

**Face restoration on degraded photos.** CodeFormer was specifically trained on damaged and low-quality faces. Topaz Photo AI's face recovery performs better on modern photography than on old printed photographs.

**Price for one-time use.** $4.99 vs $199 is a 40x cost difference. For a single family photo restoration project, ArtImageHub is the obvious choice.

**No installation.** Topaz is desktop software with significant system requirements. ArtImageHub works in a browser on any device including mobile.

---

## The Right Tool for Each Job

**Use Topaz Gigapixel AI if:**
- You're a professional photographer who needs extreme upscaling regularly
- You need to print large-format from small originals (poster size, billboard)
- You process many photos in batch workflows
- You already own the Topaz suite for other work
- Privacy requires local processing

**Use ArtImageHub if:**
- You have old family photos with scratches, fading, or physical damage
- You want face restoration on old printed portraits
- This is a one-time project, not ongoing professional work
- You need something that works in a browser without installation
- Budget matters — $4.99 vs $199

## The Honest Combination

For archival work with extremely valuable photos, some professionals use both: ArtImageHub for damage repair and face restoration, then Topaz for extreme upscaling if print size requires it.

For typical family photo restoration — a box of old prints that need to look good on screen or at 4"×6" to 8"×10" print — ArtImageHub's integrated pipeline covers everything you need.
