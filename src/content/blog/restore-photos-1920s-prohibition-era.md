1920s family photos document one of American history's most culturally dynamic decades — the flappers and jazz musicians, the speakeasies and social liberation, and the economic prosperity that preceded the Crash. These images capture families at the threshold of modernity.

## The Challenge

1920s photography was overwhelmingly black-and-white, using gelatin silver printing processes that have specific known deterioration patterns. Many 1920s portraits show the silvery metallic sheen of silver mirroring — the reflective deterioration of improperly fixed silver prints.

## How AI Helps

AI restoration handles silver mirroring by modeling the underlying image through the metallic surface layer. The algorithm reconstructs the original tonal values beneath the tarnish, recovering portraits from 1920s prints that appear obscured by their own deterioration.
