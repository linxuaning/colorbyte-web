# Restoring North African American Family Photographs

North African American family photographs — from Moroccan, Algerian, Tunisian, Libyan, and Egyptian families — document a diverse diaspora with distinctive cultural traditions reflecting the unique blend of Amazigh (Berber) indigenous culture, Arab Islamic influence, and Mediterranean connections that characterizes the Maghreb. These photographs show both the specific visual culture of North African family life and the adaptation of these traditions in American contexts.

## Cultural Heritage

North African American photographs document the specific cultural practices maintained in diaspora: the traditional foods and celebrations of North African Islam, the distinctive textile and clothing traditions (the djellaba, the kaftan, the specific embroidery styles of different regions), the music and dance traditions from different Maghreb regions, and the family structures that maintain extended family bonds across geographic distances. For North African American families, photographs document the maintenance of distinctive cultural identities within the broader American Muslim community.
