1970s family photos document the most visually distinctive decade of American consumer photography — the earth tones, the leisure suits, the wood-paneled station wagons, and the social experimentation of the 'Me Decade.' These images are simultaneously personal family history and cultural documentation.

## The Challenge

1970s color photography suffers from the characteristic deterioration of that era's consumer color printing — the magenta and yellow dyes of Kodacolor II and competing prints faded faster than the cyan dye, creating an overall green-blue color shift that makes 1970s prints instantly recognizable.

## How AI Helps

AI restoration trained on 1970s color print deterioration applies targeted correction that reverses the era-specific green-blue color shift. The recovered colors restore the genuine warmth of 1970s family photography without over-correcting into artificial brightness.
