The 1980s yuppie photograph has a specific visual grammar: the power suit, the shoulder pads, the brick mobile phone, the BMW. For families who lived through the prosperity of the Reagan-era financial boom, these photographs document a specific moment of American economic optimism.

## Material Culture of the 1980s

1980s family photographs are extraordinary documents of specific material culture: the brand clothing, the electronics (VCRs, Walkmans, the first mobile phones), the specific cars that represented status in different economic strata. This material culture was as era-specific as anything the 1950s or 1920s produced.

## The Office Photography Revolution

The 1980s saw more office and workplace photography than any previous decade — the expanding service economy produced office environments that workers photographed. Office holiday party photographs, professional milestone photographs, office interior documentation.

## Financial Optimism Archive

For families who prospered in the 1980s economy, the photographs from this period document success stories that subsequent decades may have complicated. For families who were left behind by the decade's economic transformation, the photographs document a different American story.
