Wix is one of the most popular website builders for small businesses, creative professionals, and online stores. But it has a well-known problem: image quality. Upload a photo to Wix and it compresses it automatically — sometimes aggressively — in the interest of page speed.

The result is that photos that looked sharp on your phone or computer arrive on your Wix site looking softer than you expected. The fix is not in Wix's settings. It is in the quality of the photos you upload.

## Why Does Wix Compress Photos — and Can You Stop It?

Wix uses automatic compression because page load speed directly affects search engine rankings and visitor bounce rates. A 5MB uncompressed photo would slow your site significantly; Wix compresses it to a fraction of that size and serves the compressed version.

You cannot fully disable Wix's compression. What you can control is the starting point. A photo that is already high-quality, sharp, and artifact-free before upload produces a better result after Wix's compression than a phone photo uploaded directly. The compression degrades quality proportionally — a sharper input produces a sharper output.

AI enhancement tools give your photos a quality advantage before they enter the compression pipeline.

## How Do You Enhance Photos Before Uploading to Wix?

The standard pre-upload workflow for Wix photos uses [Photo Enhancer](/photo-enhancer) as the primary step. Photo Enhancer applies SwinIR-based sharpening and Real-ESRGAN upscaling that increases both the perceived sharpness and the effective resolution of your images. After Wix's compression, the enhanced version retains significantly more detail than the unenhanced original.

For product photos taken on a phone, add a [Photo Denoiser](/photo-denoiser) pass first. Phone cameras introduce grain in any lighting condition other than bright outdoor sun, and AI denoising removes it before the sharpening amplifies it. The sequence is: Denoise → Enhance → Upload.

For photos with visible JPEG blocking artifacts — common in images you have resized, re-saved multiple times, or received from suppliers — run [JPEG Artifact Remover](/jpeg-artifact-remover) first. JPEG artifacts left in an image before sharpening become more visible after sharpening, not less. Remove them first, then sharpen.

## What About Team Photos and Headshots?

Team pages and about sections are the human face of your business on your Wix site. They deserve better than compressed phone selfies.

Even if you cannot reshoot your team photos, AI enhancement makes a significant difference. [Photo Enhancer](/photo-enhancer) recovers facial sharpness and skin detail that phone compression and Wix compression together obscure. If the original photos have motion blur — common in phone shots taken in less-than-ideal light — run them through [Photo Deblurrer](/photo-deblurrer) first to recover sharpness before the enhancement pass.

For Wix portfolio sites where team photos appear large — at 50% viewport width or more — the improvement from AI enhancement is immediately visible to visitors. At smaller sizes (sidebar headshots, small about-section photos), the difference is subtler but still present.

## How Do You Handle Historical or Archival Photos in a Wix Site?

Many small businesses, especially those in tourism, hospitality, and local services, use historical photographs of their location, community, or heritage as part of their brand story. These old photos often have significant quality issues.

For faded, cracked, or damaged historical photos intended for a Wix heritage or about page, [Old Photo Restoration](/old-photo-restoration) is the right starting point. It handles the specific degradation patterns of old photos — age-related fading, film grain, physical damage — before any sharpening pass. After restoration, run through Photo Enhancer for final sharpness before uploading to Wix.

The [Photo Colorizer](/photo-colorizer) adds color to black-and-white historical photos, producing DDColor-powered colorization that makes vintage images more visually engaging on modern web pages. This is particularly effective for local business heritage sections and tourism sites that want to present historical imagery in an accessible, modern way.

## What Are the Ideal Wix Upload Settings?

After enhancing your photos, export them with these settings before uploading to Wix:

- **Format**: JPEG for photographs, PNG for logos and graphics with transparency
- **Quality**: 85-90% JPEG quality (not maximum — larger files do not look better after Wix's own compression)
- **Width**: 2500px for hero and full-width images, 1500px for gallery images, 800px for thumbnails
- **Color profile**: sRGB (web-standard, prevents color shifts on different displays)

These settings give Wix's compression engine the best possible material to work with and produce consistently sharp results across devices.

Your Wix site is often the first professional impression potential customers have of your business. The photos deserve to look as good as possible — and AI enhancement makes that achievable without a professional photographer or retoucher.
