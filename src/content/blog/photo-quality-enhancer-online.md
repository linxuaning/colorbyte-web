## The Four Most Common Photo Quality Problems

### Problem 1: Grain and Noise
**What it looks like**: Sandy, speckled texture across the image — especially visible in dark areas. Random colored dots (red, green, blue) in shadows.

**What causes it**: High ISO settings in low light. Camera sensors amplify light signals at high ISO, and the random electrical noise in the sensor gets amplified too.

**Also caused by**: Film grain in scanned analog photos, old digital cameras at any ISO.

**The fix**: [Photo Denoiser](/photo-denoiser) — NAFNet trained on real sensor noise data removes grain while preserving image texture.

---

### Problem 2: Blur and Soft Focus
**What it looks like**: Edges that should be crisp are soft. Motion smearing in one direction. Faces or subjects that look uniformly soft or out-of-focus.

**What causes it**: Motion blur from subject or camera movement during exposure. Defocus blur from autofocus errors or shallow depth-of-field misses. Camera shake from handheld shooting in low light.

**The fix**: [Photo Deblurrer](/photo-deblurrer) — NAFNet GoPro model reconstructs missing sharpness from real motion blur patterns.

---

### Problem 3: JPEG Compression Artifacts
**What it looks like**: Visible grid of squares (blocking) in smooth areas. Halos around sharp edges. Banding in gradients. Photo looks "blocky" or "painted."

**What causes it**: Low JPEG quality settings when saving. Automatic compression by messaging apps (WhatsApp, Messenger), social media platforms, email. Re-saving a JPEG multiple times.

**The fix**: [JPEG Artifact Remover](/jpeg-artifact-remover) — SwinIR trained on real JPEG compression patterns targets and removes blocking, ringing, and banding specifically.

---

### Problem 4: Low Resolution
**What it looks like**: Photo is very small — can't print at the size you want, or displays as small on screen. Photo looks fine at its current size but pixelated when enlarged.

**What causes it**: Old low-megapixel cameras. Screen captures. Video stills. Heavy cropping. Photos downloaded at reduced sizes from websites.

**The fix**: [Photo Enhancer](/photo-enhancer) — AI super-resolution upscales by 2–4× with learned detail reconstruction better than standard interpolation.

## The Right Order When Multiple Problems Are Present

If your photo has more than one problem — which is common — process in this order:

**1. JPEG Artifact Removal first**
Compression artifacts are structural — they're baked into the image's pixel values. Removing them gives subsequent steps a cleaner base image.

**2. Noise/Grain Reduction second**
Denoising from a clean, artifact-free image produces better results than denoising an image with compression artifacts still present.

**3. Deblurring third**
Blur removal from a clean, noise-free image works better than trying to deblur and denoise simultaneously.

**4. Upscaling last**
Always upscale from the cleanest possible version of the image. Upscaling grain, artifacts, or blur amplifies them.
