# Restoring Thai American Heritage Photographs

Thai American family photographs document a community with distinctive Buddhist cultural traditions and a specific immigration history shaped by the relationship between Thailand and the United States in the post-WWII era. Thai Americans include both families who came through professional immigration pathways and those with connections to American military presence in Thailand. Photographs from Thai American families document both the specific cultural traditions of Thailand and the adaptation of these traditions in American contexts.

## Cultural Heritage

Thai American photographs document the specific Buddhist practices that are central to Thai cultural life: the temple (wat) as the community center, the specific Buddhist ceremonies and observances that structure the ritual calendar, the merit-making practices (making offerings to monks, participating in temple activities) that express Buddhist values. For Thai American families, temple photographs document the community built around Buddhist practice that maintains Thai cultural identity in American contexts.
