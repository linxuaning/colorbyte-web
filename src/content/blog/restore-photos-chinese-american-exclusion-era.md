Chinese American exclusion-era photos document the resilience of families who maintained community identity despite systematic legal exclusion. Chinatown community organization photos, paper son documentation images, and family portraits preserved during the exclusion era are rare historical documents.

## The Challenge

Chinese American exclusion-era photos were often stored with particular care because they served as documentation as well as family memory. However, even careful storage over a century results in significant deterioration, and many photos show the color shift and fading of very old prints.

## How AI Helps

AI restoration applies century-appropriate treatment to exclusion-era photos, which often require more aggressive recovery algorithms than more recent images. The historical significance of these photos makes maximum recovery effort particularly important.
