## Understanding Focus Problems in Old Photos

Different causes of blur require different approaches for optimal improvement.

### Types of Blur

**Focus Blur**:
- Subject outside depth of field
- Camera focused on wrong point
- Shallow depth of field
- Common in vintage photos

**Camera Shake**:
- Movement during exposure
- Long shutter speeds
- Unstable camera support
- Often combined with focus blur

**Subject Motion**:
- Moving subjects during exposure
- Partial blur (moving parts)
- Ghost-like effects
- Often unavoidable with slow film

### Why Old Photos Are Often Soft

| Factor | Impact | Prevalence |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Slow film speeds | Required wide apertures | Very common |
| Manual focusing | Accuracy varied | Common |
| Simple cameras | Limited focus control | Very common |
| Long exposures | Any movement caused blur | Common |

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can AI actually add detail to out-of-focus photos?**

Short answer: AI doesn't add real detail but can intelligently enhance existing information and predict likely detail patterns based on training data. Our [AI restoration tool](/enhance-old-photos) produces significantly clearer-looking results while maintaining authenticity. The image appears sharper even though original focus can't be changed.

**Why do old photos seem softer than modern photos?**

Short answer: Slow film required wide apertures (thin depth of field), manual focus was imprecise, camera lenses were simpler, and long exposures captured any movement. Print paper and processing also affected apparent sharpness. These were technical limitations, not photographer error.

**How much can a severely blurry photo be improved?**

Short answer: Results vary with blur severity. Slight softness can be dramatically improved; moderate blur shows notable enhancement; severe blur allows only modest improvement. Our AI provides meaningful enhancement for most photos, but truly unrecognizable images have fundamental limits.
