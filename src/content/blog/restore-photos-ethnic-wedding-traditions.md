# Restoring Ethnic and Cultural Wedding Photos: Marriage Traditions Across Cultures

The culturally specific wedding ceremony preserves traditions that go back generations — the specific rituals, the specific clothing, the specific ceremonial elements that distinguish one community's wedding practice from the mainstream American wedding.

## Ritual Documentation

The specific rituals of culturally specific weddings — the Greek Orthodox crowning ceremony, the Hindu fire ritual, the Jewish glass-breaking, the Nigerian traditional ceremony — are documented in wedding photographs with particular historical significance. These rituals are the living practices of ancient traditions.

## Traditional Clothing

The traditional wedding clothing in culturally specific ceremonies — the saree, the hanbok, the kimono worn at intercultural weddings, the African kente cloth — creates a specific archive of textile heritage. Restoration that recovers clothing detail preserves this cultural documentation.

## Bi-Cultural Weddings

For families navigating bi-cultural identities, wedding photographs that combine traditional and contemporary elements document the specific cultural negotiation of their heritage. These photographs are particularly interesting as social documents.

## Getting the Best Results

Start with the highest-quality scan you can produce — 600 DPI minimum for standard prints, 1200 DPI for small prints or photographs with faces you want to identify. Color mode scanning, even for black-and-white photographs, gives AI restoration algorithms more information to work with.

After restoration, compare the result with the original at full zoom. Check faces carefully to ensure identity is preserved, and note any areas where AI may have filled in damaged sections with plausible but uncertain reconstructions.
