# Restoring Appalachian Mountain Heritage Photographs

Appalachian mountain communities developed distinctive cultural traditions over centuries of relative geographic isolation: unique musical traditions, craft practices, dialects, and community structures that have been documented photographically since the late 19th century. Family photographs from Appalachian communities capture this distinctive heritage alongside universal family themes.

## The Cultural Context

Photographs from Appalachian communities document specific visual contexts: the specific architecture of mountain homesteads built for the landscape and climate, the craft practices of basket-making and quilting and woodworking, the musical instruments built by hand, and the community gatherings organized around church and family that structured social life.
