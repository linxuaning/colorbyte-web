# Restoring Grange and Farm Organization Photographs

The Patrons of Husbandry (the Grange) was founded in 1867 and became one of the most important rural community organizations in American history, providing both economic mutual aid (cooperative purchasing and marketing) and social community for farm families who were often geographically isolated. Photographs from Grange activities document both the economic functions of agricultural cooperation and the social life of rural communities organized around these institutions.

## Agricultural Heritage

Grange photographs document the specific activities of agricultural community organization: the Grange hall gatherings that brought farm families together for both business and social purposes, the specific activities of Grange chapters (the debates, the cooperative projects, the educational programs), and the community celebrations that Granges organized for their rural membership. These photographs show the organizational forms through which farm families maintained community across geographic distances.

## Rural Community Documentation

Grange and farm organization photographs face preservation challenges related to their rural origins: the temperature extremes of agricultural environments, the limited access to archival materials in rural communities, and the storage conditions of Grange halls that were used for functions rather than preservation. AI restoration addresses these preservation challenges, recovering the visual documentation of agricultural community institutions.
