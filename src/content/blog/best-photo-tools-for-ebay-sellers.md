## Why Does Photo Quality Matter So Much on eBay?

eBay is a visual marketplace. Unlike Amazon, where listings for new items often use manufacturer photos, eBay primarily sells used, vintage, and unique items that buyers cannot see anywhere else. Every buyer is making a judgment call about condition, authenticity, and value based purely on your photos.

Blurry photos introduce doubt. When a buyer cannot clearly see the details of an item — the condition of a clasp on a necklace, the sharpness of an engraving, whether a garment has pilling — they either pass on the listing or ask questions that slow the sale. Clear, sharp photos eliminate doubt and accelerate buying decisions.

eBay's own data shows that listings with high-resolution photos using the zoom feature convert better. The zoom tool requires large, sharp images to be useful — photos that are small or blurry show buyers a pixelated mess when they hover.

## What Are the Most Common Photo Problems for eBay Sellers?

**Motion blur** is the most common issue. Holding a phone while photographing a small item almost guarantees some camera shake, especially in indoor lighting that forces the camera to use a slower shutter speed.

**Focus blur** happens when the camera autofocuses on the background instead of the item, or when photographing items with uneven depth — a wristwatch face that's in focus but the strap blurring, for example.

**Noise and grain** appear in photos taken in dim indoor conditions. The camera compensates with high ISO sensitivity, adding visible grain that makes photos look cheap.

**Low resolution** is common when sellers upload photos taken on older phones or when photos have been copied and re-sent through messaging apps that compress images automatically.
