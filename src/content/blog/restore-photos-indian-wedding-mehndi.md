# Restoring South Asian Indian Wedding Photographs

South Asian Indian wedding photographs document one of the most elaborate and visually spectacular wedding traditions in American family photography: the multi-day celebration that includes the mehndi (henna) ceremony, the sangeet (music and dance evening), the baraat (groom's procession), and the main wedding ceremony with its specific Hindu or Sikh or other religious rituals. For Indian American families, wedding photographs document the maintenance of elaborate wedding traditions in American contexts while adapting to American schedules and venues.

## Cultural Heritage

Indian wedding photographs document the extraordinary visual richness of South Asian wedding culture: the specific bridal attire (the lehenga or sari in specific traditional colors), the groom's distinctive wedding dress (often a sherwani or achkan), the elaborate floral decorations, the specific ritual objects of the ceremony (the sacred fire, the wedding garlands, the specific ritual items from regional traditions), and the hundreds of family members and guests who typically attend multi-day wedding celebrations.

## Wedding Documentation

Indian wedding photographs face preservation challenges related to the diverse conditions of Indian American household storage and to the specific photography conditions of elaborate indoor ceremonies with complex lighting.
