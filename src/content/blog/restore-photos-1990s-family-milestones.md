# Restoring 1990s Family Milestone Photos: Before Digital Changed Everything

The 1990s were the last decade of mainstream consumer film photography. Families who had graduations, weddings, and births in the 1990s documented them on film — and those film photographs are now showing 30-35 years of aging.

## The Last Film Decade

1990s consumer photography represented the maturity of film technology and the beginning of its displacement. The films of the 1990s were the best-ever consumer films in many technical respects — more stable dyes, finer grain, better color accuracy than any previous decade. But 'more stable' still doesn't mean 'permanent.'

## One-Hour Photo Lab Processing

The one-hour photo lab was ubiquitous in the 1990s — every drugstore and mall had one. The convenience was real, but the quality control varied. Some 1990s photographs from one-hour labs are showing more deterioration than expected because of processing shortcuts.

## Milestone Photography in the 1990s

Weddings, graduations, births, and family reunions from the 1990s were photographed in earnest. These photographs represent the last generation of milestone events documented on film before digital changed the economics and practices of family photography.
