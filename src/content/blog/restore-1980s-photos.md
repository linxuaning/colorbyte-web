## 1980s Photography Characteristics

### Film and Camera Types

**35mm Point-and-Shoot:**
- Compact cameras became mainstream
- Auto-focus and auto-exposure
- Variable quality results

**Disposable Cameras:**
- Introduced late 1980s
- Often lower image quality
- Popular at events and parties

**Disc Film (1982-1999):**
- Small negatives, grainy results
- Popular briefly
- Challenging to restore

### Common Quality Issues

**Typical Problems:**
- Flash red-eye
- Soft focus (cheap lenses)
- Color shifts from aging
- One-hour lab processing variations

## Scanning 1980s Photos

### Best Practices

- Standard 300-600 DPI works well
- Check for stuck photos in albums
- Handle magnetic album pages carefully
- Scan both prints and negatives if available

### Album Preservation Issues

Many 1980s photos are stuck in "magnetic" photo albums with sticky pages. These can damage photos—consider professional removal if photos are stuck.
