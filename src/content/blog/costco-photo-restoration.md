If you're a Costco member wondering whether Costco Photo Center can restore your old family photos, here's the direct answer:

**Costco Photo Center is a photo printing service — it does not offer photo restoration.** Like Walgreens Photo and CVS Photo, Costco prints your digital files at high quality. It doesn't repair damage, reconstruct faces, or correct fading in old photographs.

What makes Costco worth knowing about: it has some of the best print quality among retail photo services, and its pricing is competitive. For printing a restored photo, Costco Photo Center is a strong option.

---

## What Costco Photo Center Offers

**Standard photo prints:** High-quality prints at competitive prices. Costco uses professional-grade printers and paper. Print quality is generally considered among the best available at retail price points.

**Large format prints:** Posters up to 20"×30", canvas prints, and other large format options. For displaying a restored family portrait, Costco's large format options are worth considering.

**Photo books and gifts:** Albums, calendars, mugs, and other photo products.

**Basic online editing:** Crop, rotate, and minor adjustments before ordering. These are cosmetic tools, not restoration.

**Same-day pickup:** Available at most Costco Photo Centers during store hours.

**Requires Costco membership:** Unlike Walgreens or CVS, you need an active Costco membership ($65/year basic, $130/year Executive) to use Costco Photo.

## The Complete Workflow: Restored Photo to Costco Print

**Step 1: Digitize the original print**

If you have a physical photo:
- **Best quality:** Flatbed scanner at 600–1200 DPI (many public libraries have free flatbed scanners)
- **Good quality:** Phone + Microsoft Lens or Google PhotoScan (free apps)
- **Minimum:** Direct phone photo in even, shadow-free light

Upload the restored HD file to costco.com/photo or the Costco Photo app:
- Choose size (8"×10" is ~$3.99 — ideal for framing)

**Total cost:** $4.99 restoration + $4–15 for Costco print = high-quality restored print

## Why Costco Print Quality Matters for Restored Photos

When you've done the work of properly restoring an old family photo, print quality matters more than it does for a casual snapshot. Costco's prints hold up better over time and show more detail in shadow areas — both relevant for a restored portrait that you're framing.

For large format (16"×20" or larger), the quality difference between Costco and drugstore kiosks is more pronounced. If you're doing a significant restoration — a WWII service portrait, a 50th anniversary reprint — Costco's large format print quality is worth the membership requirement.
