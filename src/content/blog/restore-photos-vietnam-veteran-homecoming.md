# Preserving Vietnam Veteran Homecoming and Return Photos

Vietnam veteran homecoming photos capture one of history's most complicated returns — soldiers who came home to a divided nation, often without the celebration their sacrifice deserved. These photos document the reunion of families separated by a controversial war.

## The Challenge

Late 1960s and early 1970s color photography used Kodacolor processes with known fading characteristics — particularly the loss of cyan dye that makes these prints shift toward warm red-orange tones over time.

## How AI Helps

AI restoration includes specific correction profiles for Kodacolor and other 1960s-1970s color processes. The systematic cyan loss is reversed by the targeted color correction algorithm, recovering the accurate skin tones and colors of veteran homecoming photos.

## Next Steps

Gather your photographs, scan at the highest resolution available (600 DPI minimum), and visit [PhotoFix](/old-photo-restoration) to start the restoration process. AI tools can handle damage that once required expensive professional services, making preservation accessible for every family.
