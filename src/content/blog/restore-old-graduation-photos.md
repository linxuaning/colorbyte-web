## Common Damage Patterns

### Portrait-Specific Damage

**Cap and Gown Issues**:
- Black fabric shows fading first
- Tassel colors fade
- Honor cords lose distinction
- Fine details obscured

**Background Deterioration**:
- Studio backdrops fade unevenly
- Lighting effects diminish
- Contrast between subject and background lost

### Ceremony Photo Challenges

**Environmental Factors**:
- Indoor lighting variations
- Flash reflections
- Motion blur from movement
- Crowd obscuring subjects

**Physical Damage**:
- Handling over years
- Display in frames without UV glass
- Album adhesive damage
- Water or humidity exposure

Short answer: AI restoration can enhance multiple faces simultaneously. For large group photos, expect some variation in results across different faces. **Can colorized graduation photos be historically accurate?**

Short answer: Largely yes. Academic regalia colors are well-documented—universities maintain color standards over time. Yearbooks and university archives can provide references. Our [colorization tool](/colorize-photos-free) can add appropriate colors based on the institution's documented regalia colors.

**How do I find out what academic honors my ancestor earned from their graduation photo?**

Short answer: Look for honor cords, stoles, or hood colors that indicate achievements. Research the institution's regalia traditions for that era. Compare to yearbooks or commencement programs from the same year. University archives can often help interpret academic insignia.

**Should I restore each graduation photo or create a composite family graduation display?**

Short answer: Restore individual photos first for best quality. Then you can create compilations showing educational progress—kindergarten through doctorate, or generations of family graduates. Our tool works on individual images, which can then be arranged into displays.
