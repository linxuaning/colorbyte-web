## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can newspaper obituary photos be restored to clear images?**

Short answer: Significant improvement is usually possible. Our [AI restoration tool](/enhance-old-photos) removes halftone dot patterns and enhances facial detail. Results won't match original photo quality, but improvement is typically dramatic—making faces clearly recognizable that were previously obscured by printing patterns.

**How do I restore a memorial photo that's the only image of a deceased relative?**

Short answer: Handle with extreme care. Scan at highest possible resolution before any physical handling. Use our AI restoration for enhancement—it's non-destructive to the original. Consider professional digitization for very fragile items. Create multiple backups of both original scan and restored version.

**Should I try to find the original photo used for an obituary?**

Short answer: Yes, if possible. Originals always produce better results than newspaper reproductions. Check with family members who may have the source photo, contact funeral homes that sometimes keep records, or search for other photos of the same person that might be better quality.

**Can post-mortem Victorian photographs be restored?**

Short answer: Yes, the same restoration techniques apply. These historically significant photos deserve respectful treatment. Many families don't realize they have post-mortem photography—it was common practice. AI restoration can improve visibility while maintaining the dignity of these memorial images.

**How do I share restored memorial photos with extended family?**

Short answer: Digital sharing makes this easy. Create a shared folder or photo album online. Consider memorial websites like Find A Grave or family tree sites that accept photos. Funeral home websites sometimes archive memorial images. Family group chats or email work for immediate family.
