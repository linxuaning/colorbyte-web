# Restoring Polish Christmas Tradition Photographs

Polish Wigilia (Christmas Eve) celebrations have a distinctive character that Polish American families maintain with particular intensity: the twelve-course meatless meal (representing the twelve apostles), the hay placed under the tablecloth (remembering the manger), the Oplatek (Christmas wafer) shared among family members with wishes and forgiveness, and the specific Polish traditional foods that constitute the meal. Photographs from Wigilia celebrations document the maintenance of one of the most elaborate Catholic Christmas traditions in American domestic life.

## Cultural Heritage

Wigilia photographs document the specific ritual elements that distinguish Polish Christmas Eve from generic American Christmas: the specific placement of the traditional foods, the Oplatek sharing ceremony that is one of the most emotionally meaningful elements of the celebration, the specific traditional dishes (barszcz czerwony, pierogi, carp or herring), and the family gathering that makes this meal the most significant of the year. For Polish American families, Wigilia photographs document cultural and religious heritage maintained across generations in American contexts.
