# Preserving Dominican and Haitian American Heritage Photos

Dominican and Haitian American photos document the cultural heritage of Caribbean immigrant communities — the merengue and kompa music traditions, the Catholic and Vodou religious practices, the family networks that span both islands and American cities.

## The Challenge

Caribbean immigrant family photos face the specific preservation challenges of tropical origin environments. Photos stored in Haiti and the Dominican Republic before emigration were subject to high-humidity damage, tropical pests, and the fragility of tropical storage conditions.

## How AI Helps

AI restoration approaches tropical-climate-damaged photos with humidity damage protocols that address the distinctive mold and silver migration patterns of photos stored in warm, wet environments. The restoration recovers maximum detail from images that tropical conditions severely degraded.

## Next Steps

Gather your photographs, scan at the highest resolution available (600 DPI minimum), and visit [PhotoFix](/old-photo-restoration) to start the restoration process. AI tools can handle damage that once required expensive professional services, making preservation accessible for every family.
