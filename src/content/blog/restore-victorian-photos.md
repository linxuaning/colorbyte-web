## Types of Victorian Photography

### Common Formats

**Daguerreotypes (1840s-1860s):**
- Mirror-like metal surface
- One-of-a-kind images
- Highly valuable
- Requires professional digitization

**Tintypes (1850s-1900s):**
- Metal plate base
- More durable than paper
- Often informal portraits
- Common at fairs and events

**Cabinet Cards (1860s-1900s):**
- Card-mounted photographs
- Standard portrait format
- Studio photographer stamps
- Most common Victorian format

**CDVs (Cartes de Visite):**
- Smaller card format
- Often collected and traded
- Multiple poses common

## Scanning Antique Photos

**Best Practices:**
- Handle with cotton gloves
- Never remove from original mounts
- Scan at 1200+ DPI
- Photograph daguerreotypes at angle to avoid reflection
