When a family member passes away, the photographs that go into memorial displays, funeral programs, and memorial books often haven't been looked at in decades — and they show it. Restoring these photographs quickly, often within 24–48 hours, is something AI restoration now makes possible.

---

## The Time Constraint

Memorial services are often planned within days of a death. If the family wants restored photographs for:

- A display board or portrait at the service
- A funeral program or booklet
- A slide presentation
- A memorial book for guests

The timeline is typically 48–96 hours from decision to printed materials.

**AI restoration: 30–90 seconds per photo.** The bottleneck is scanning, not restoration.

---

## Quick Digitization Options

**Phone photography:** For immediate needs, photographing a print with a phone is fast enough. Use Google PhotoScan (free iOS/Android) to reduce glare on reflective prints. Shoot in good indirect lighting, parallel to the print.

**Scanner:** If accessible, 600 DPI flatbed scan produces better quality. Libraries have scanners; some may have extended hours.

**For very urgent situations:** A clear phone photograph in good lighting → ArtImageHub restoration → print at a local same-hour service (Walgreens, CVS, Walmart Photo, or FedEx/Staples for larger prints) completes in a few hours.

## Workflow for Memorial Photo Restoration

**Step 1: Gather the photos**  
Look for clear portraits — not group photos where the subject is small. Portraits where the subject's face is clearly the focus produce the best restored results. If the family has multiple photos, restoration is fast enough to process several and select the best results.

**Step 2: Photograph or scan**  
For the most important photos, scan if possible. For speed, phone photography in good light works.

**Step 3: Upload and restore**  
ArtImageHub: $4.99 per photo, 30–90 seconds. Multiple photos can be processed sequentially or simultaneously in separate browser tabs.

**Step 4: Download and print**  
Download HD restored files → print at nearest location.

## What Photo Restoration Does for Memorial Portraits

The most common situation: a 1950s–1970s portrait that is faded, yellowed, and has soft faces. After AI restoration:

- **CodeFormer** reconstructs face detail — the person becomes clearly recognizable rather than vaguely outlined
- **GFPGAN** corrects the yellowing and fading — the portrait looks as it would have when it was taken
- **Real-ESRGAN** upscales for printing — produces a clean 8×10 or 11×14 without pixelation

For a memorial, the difference between a faded, barely-recognizable portrait and a clearly restored one is significant for everyone who attends.
