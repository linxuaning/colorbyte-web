# AI Photo Restoration vs Photoshop: Which Is Better for Old Photos?

When it comes to restoring cherished old photographs, two approaches dominate the conversation: AI-powered tools like PhotoFix and traditional manual editing in Photoshop. Both can produce beautiful results, but they serve different needs, skill levels, and time budgets. Understanding the real-world differences helps you choose the right tool for your specific photos.

## Where Photoshop Excels

Adobe Photoshop remains the gold standard for professional retouchers who need complete creative control. With Photoshop, a skilled editor can painstakingly reconstruct missing facial features, paint in entire backgrounds, and make nuanced artistic decisions about how a restored photo should look. For extremely high-value or complex restorations — a single treasured portrait where every detail matters — a human Photoshop expert may produce the most accurate result. The tradeoff is time (often hours per photo) and cost (professional retouching can run $50–$300+ per image).

## Where AI Restoration Wins

For the vast majority of people with boxes of faded, scratched, or water-damaged family photos, AI restoration offers a compelling alternative. AI models trained on millions of photographs understand what aged photos are supposed to look like — they can identify and correct foxing, fading, creases, and color shift automatically. PhotoFix processes an image in seconds rather than hours, at a fraction of the cost. When you have 50 old photos from your grandmother's attic, AI makes restoring all of them practical rather than prohibitively expensive.

## Hybrid Workflows: The Best of Both

Many professional restorers now use AI as a first pass before applying manual Photoshop refinements. The AI handles the tedious grunt work — noise removal, color correction, basic scratch repair — while the human editor focuses on high-level decisions: expression accuracy, background reconstruction, and final color grading. For families who want the best possible result on a particularly meaningful photo, this hybrid approach delivers professional quality at much lower cost than pure manual work.

## Practical Steps to Get Started

Before uploading your photo, take a moment to gently clean the surface with a soft, dry cloth to remove loose dust or debris. Scan at the highest resolution your equipment allows — 600 DPI is a solid baseline, but 1200 DPI or higher yields noticeably better restoration results. Save the scan as a TIFF or PNG rather than JPEG to preserve every detail.

Once you have a clean digital copy, visit [PhotoFix](/old-photo-restoration) and upload your image. The AI analyzes each pixel in context, identifying which degradation patterns to correct while preserving the authentic character of the original. Within seconds you'll see a preview of the restored version, and you can download the full-resolution result ready for printing or sharing.
