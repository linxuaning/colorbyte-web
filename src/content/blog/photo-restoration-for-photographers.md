## How Should You Price Restoration as a Studio Add-On?

The most effective pricing structure for photographers adds restoration as a tiered upsell to existing packages.

**Standalone restoration**: $40-100 per image depending on damage level. Colorization as an add-on: +$35-60 per image.

**Legacy package**: New portrait session (your standard rate) plus restoration of up to 10 family photos at a package price of $300-600. This positions restoration as part of a meaningful family project rather than a transactional service.

**Heirloom album**: Restored photos printed in a professional photo book alongside new portraits. Full package pricing $500-1,200 depending on album size and restoration volume.

The legacy and heirloom packages consistently command higher prices than standalone restoration because they create a meaningful context for the work.

## What Is the Optimal Scanning Setup for Studio Use?

Physical print scanning is the core technical skill addition. Most families bring actual prints rather than already-digitized files.

The Epson Perfection V600 handles standard prints, 35mm negatives, and slides at sufficient resolution for AI processing. Scan 4x6 prints at 600-1200 DPI. Scan smaller originals (wallet size, 2x3 prints) at 2400 DPI minimum to provide the AI models adequate pixel data.

Before scanning, examine originals for physical fragility. Cracking, embrittlement, or active flaking requires gentle handling. Use cotton gloves. Do not flex brittle prints. For very fragile originals, photograph them under raking light with your camera rather than placing them on a flatbed scanner.

## Does AI Restoration Replace or Supplement Your Existing Skills?

AI restoration handles predictable degradation patterns — grain, fading, soft focus, uniform cracking — extremely well. It is genuinely a replacement for the Photoshop time those tasks would previously require.

Complex damage — large missing areas, faces obscured by severe deterioration, intricate background reconstruction — still benefits from human judgment. After running the AI restoration pass, examine the output at 100% zoom. The model will handle most damage automatically. For any regions that still need work, targeted Photoshop correction on the AI-enhanced base (rather than the original degraded scan) is dramatically faster than manual restoration from scratch.

Most photographers find that 80-90% of restoration jobs require no manual intervention after AI processing. The remaining 10-20% need 10-30 minutes of Photoshop work.

## How Do You Market Restoration Without Building a New Audience?

Your existing client base is the primary market. Announce the service via email to past clients. Post before-and-after examples on your social media. Include a restoration option in your booking confirmation and inquiry response emails.

For new client acquisition specifically for restoration, Google searches for "photo restoration [city name]" generate high-intent traffic. A simple page on your existing studio website optimized for this search term captures local restoration demand at minimal cost.

Funeral homes are worth a direct outreach effort. They regularly need portrait restorations on urgent timelines — 24-48 hours between a family's request and when the image appears at a service. A studio that can reliably deliver in that window becomes a trusted partner. Contact 10-15 local funeral homes directly with your service offering and a sample restoration.

## Are There Workflow Tools That Help Manage Restoration Orders?

For low volume (under 20 orders per month), simple folder organization and email is sufficient. Create a folder per client order, maintain original scans and AI-enhanced outputs separately, and deliver via a shared Google Drive or Dropbox link.

For higher volume, a simple project management tool — even a shared spreadsheet tracking order status — prevents jobs from falling through gaps. Track: client name, intake date, images received, processing status, delivery date, return of originals.

The most important operational practice: never modify the original scan. Always work from a copy. The original unmodified scan is the archival baseline; the AI-enhanced version is the derivative. If a client later wants different processing or a different approach, you can restart from the original.

Your camera skills, your client relationships, and your understanding of what families value in photographs — combined with AI tools that compress the technical work into minutes — create a service addition worth building.
