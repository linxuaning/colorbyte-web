The 1990s grunge and alternative scene was photographed on the cusp of the digital transition — mostly on film, often by participants, documenting a youth culture that was simultaneously mainstream and subcultural.

## The Film Documentation of Grunge

1990s alternative music scene photographs were made on the last generation of consumer film — 35mm, mostly, in the hands of young photographers who were participants in the scenes they documented. The photographs have the specific look of film photography at its most accessible: sharp when conditions allowed, grainy in the clubs and shows where most of the documentation happened.

## Seattle and the Spread

The Seattle grunge scene, and the alternative music scenes it influenced nationally, are documented in personal photographs of fans, musicians, and community members. These photographs capture specific venues, specific people, specific moments in a cultural movement.

## Film Before Digital

The 1990s alternative scene was the last major youth cultural movement documented primarily on film. The transition to digital photography happened during the early 2000s, and the grunge era exists in film's characteristic light. Restoration recovers this aesthetic accurately.
