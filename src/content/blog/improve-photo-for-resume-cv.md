## Do You Even Need a Photo on Your CV?

### Which Markets Require or Expect CV Photos?

The answer varies dramatically by country, and getting it wrong can work against you.

**Include a photo** in applications for Germany, Austria, Switzerland, France, Spain, Italy, the Netherlands, Belgium, and most other continental European markets. In these countries, a photo-free CV can read as unusual or evasive, though it is rarely disqualifying.

**Include a photo** for all applications in Japan, South Korea, China, Taiwan, Hong Kong, Singapore, and most Southeast Asian markets. In Japan especially, CV photo requirements are highly formalized — some industries specify exact dimensions, background colors, and even recent date of photo (taken within three months).

**Omit the photo** for applications in the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, and Australia. In these markets, including a photo can raise concerns about discrimination law compliance and may cause your CV to be rejected preemptively to avoid legal risk.

**Check market norms individually** for the Middle East, Latin America, and emerging markets — conventions vary by country and industry.

If you are applying across multiple markets simultaneously, the pragmatic approach is to maintain two versions of your CV: one with a high-quality photo for EU/APAC applications and one without for US/UK applications.

## What Makes a CV Photo Look Unprofessional?

There are four distinct technical problems that make a CV photo undermine rather than support your application — and each has a different cause and a different fix.

### Low Resolution

A photo that looks acceptable on your phone screen may be only 800×600 pixels — which is fine for digital display but prints as a blurry, pixelated postage stamp on an A4 CV. This is especially common when the original photo was cropped aggressively from a group shot or screenshotted from a social platform. The [photo enhancer](/photo-enhancer) uses Real-ESRGAN upscaling to increase the pixel dimensions while recovering fine detail — facial features, collar edges, hair texture — rather than simply stretching the image.

### Digital Grain from Indoor Lighting

The most common source of CV photos is a phone camera shot indoors — in an office, at home, at an event. Without strong natural light, the phone sensor raises its ISO, and the result is a speckled, rough texture visible across the background, skin, and clothing. This grain is distinct from blur; a grainy photo can be in perfect focus and still look like a low-quality image. The [photo denoiser](/photo-denoiser) uses the NAFNet model to separate real skin texture and fabric detail from random sensor noise, removing only the noise.

### JPEG Compression Artifacts

If you received your photo via WhatsApp, saved it from a messaging thread, or took a screenshot, it has been aggressively compressed. The tell-tale signs: blocky square patterns in flat areas (the background, your suit or jacket), a smeared quality around high-contrast edges like hairlines and collar lines, and an overall "crushed" look. The [JPEG artifact remover](/jpeg-artifact-remover) uses the SwinIR model to identify and smooth these compression patterns while restoring clean edges.

### Blur from Camera Shake or Missed Focus

A photo where your face is soft — not grainy, but genuinely blurry — usually means the camera focused on the background instead of your face, or that the camera or subject moved slightly during the shot. This is the trickiest problem to fix in post because the detail was never captured. For mild blur, the [photo deblurrer](/photo-deblurrer) can recover meaningful sharpness. For severe blur where facial features are not distinguishable, retaking the photo is faster than trying to fix it.

## What Are the CV Photo Requirements in Different Contexts?

**Formal European CV (Germany, Austria, Switzerland)**: Typically 3.5×4.5 cm, full-face portrait, neutral or white background, business attire. The photo is often placed in the top-right corner of the first page. Quality expectations are high — a blurry or pixelated photo is read as unprofessional.

**Business casual (France, Netherlands, startup context)**: More flexibility on attire and background (light grey or off-white works), but technical quality is still expected. Smiling is more common in these markets than in formal German applications.

**Japan/Korea formal**: Often requires photos of very specific dimensions (typically 3×4 cm in Japan), taken with a plain blue or white background, and the photo should be taken within three months of submission. In Japan, dedicated photo booths (証明写真機) produce the correct format. AI enhancement of a high-quality photo from these booths is effective for improving resolution or grain.

**LinkedIn**: Square format, face takes up 60–70% of the frame, photo survives circular cropping. Expressions can be warmer and more approachable than a formal CV photo.

## How Do You Take an Acceptable DIY CV Photo at Home?

If you need to start from scratch without a photographer, these settings and conditions produce consistently usable results:

**Background**: Stand in front of a plain wall — white, light grey, or soft neutral. Remove anything visible in the frame behind you. A curtain, a door with visible hardware, or a bookshelf all read as unprofessional.

**Light**: Face a window with natural daylight. The window should be to your side or slightly in front of you — never behind you, which creates silhouetting. Overcast daylight is ideal (softer, no harsh shadows). Shoot midday on a slightly cloudy day for the most forgiving conditions.

**Framing**: Hold the phone at eye level or an inch above. Never shoot from below. Frame so your face fills roughly the top half of the frame with a small amount of space above your head.

**Expression and attire**: Wear what you would wear to an interview for this role. A relaxed, composed expression with a slight smile works across most industries. Avoid a toothless expressionless shot (reads as tense) or a wide grin (reads as casual for formal markets).

**Shoot in burst mode**: Take 30–40 frames with a 2-second timer and pick the sharpest, most natural-looking frame. Even a minor head movement during the shot creates enough blur to matter.

## When Should You Invest in a Professional Headshot vs Use AI Enhancement?

AI enhancement produces results that are difficult to distinguish from professional photography when the source photo is technically sound (in focus, reasonably lit) and only needs quality improvements. If your photo passes the "can you clearly see who this is, and do they look composed?" test, AI can handle the rest.

Invest in a professional photographer when: the fundamental conditions of your photo cannot be fixed by AI (severe unflattering light, wrong background that you cannot remove, expression that does not represent you well), or when the application context is very high-stakes (senior executive roles, competitive European markets where the CV photo carries meaningful weight).

For most job seekers — especially those applying to mid-level roles, startup environments, or markets where the photo is a nice-to-have rather than a formal requirement — fixing a decent phone photo with the [photo enhancer](/photo-enhancer) is the practical and cost-effective path.
