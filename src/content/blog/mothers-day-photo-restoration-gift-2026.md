## How to Restore a Family Photo for Mother's Day

### Step 1: Find the Photo

Look for photos that meant something to your mom — her childhood, her parents, her wedding day, early family life. The older and more damaged, the more dramatic the restoration will be.

Ask relatives if they have originals. Old prints have more detail than digital copies made years ago.

### Step 2: Scan It

You don't need a scanner. Use your phone:

1. Open Microsoft Lens (free, iOS/Android) or Apple's document scanner (Notes app)
2. Lay the photo flat in good light
3. Shoot from directly above at the highest resolution
4. Export as JPEG

## What AI Photo Restoration Can Fix

- **Scratches and tears:** Physical damage from decades of handling
- **Fading:** Chemical degradation that turns prints yellow or drains color
- **Blurry faces:** Old cameras often had soft focus; AI reconstructs face detail
- **Water damage:** Staining and tide marks from floods or damp storage
- **Low contrast:** Flat, washed-out tones restored to natural-looking depth

## Realistic Expectations

AI restoration works best when there's underlying image information to recover. Very badly torn photos with large missing sections are harder. Severely damaged photos may still show some damage after restoration.

For typical family photos — faded, scratched, or yellowed — the results are often remarkable. Most people describe seeing a restored version of an old family photo as emotional.
