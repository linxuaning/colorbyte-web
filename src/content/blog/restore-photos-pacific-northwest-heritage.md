Pacific Northwest photographs document some of America's most dramatic landscapes — the rainforests, the Cascade peaks, the rocky coastlines — and the industries that organized European-American settlement: logging, fishing, farming in the valleys, and the maritime commerce of the Puget Sound and Columbia River. Family photographs from the Pacific Northwest capture both the beauty of the landscape and the hard physical work that defined early settler life in the region.

## Regional Context

The specific industries of the Pacific Northwest — old-growth logging that produced photographs of men standing on springboards beside Douglas firs 8 feet in diameter, commercial salmon fishing that documented the abundance of Northwest rivers before dam construction, and wheat farming in the Columbia Plateau's dry eastern regions — created distinctive photographic genres. These photographs show the specific equipment, clothing, and working conditions of industries that were central to regional identity.

## Industry Documentation

Pacific Northwest photographs face preservation challenges related to the region's climate: the high humidity of the coastal zones, the significant rainfall that creates the conditions for mold growth, and the temperature variations between the coast and the interior. Photographs stored in coastal Pacific Northwest environments often show specific mold and humidity damage patterns that AI restoration addresses particularly effectively.
