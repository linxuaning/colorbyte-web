## Why Do Real Estate Agents Have Old Photo Problems?

Three situations come up repeatedly:

**Older digital photos from previous listings.** A property was last listed in 2009, 2014, or 2018. The listing photos exist somewhere in a file or MLS archive, but they were taken on cameras with 8- or 12-megapixel sensors that now look soft compared to modern 24–45 megapixel DSLR shots. If the property is relisting without a significant remodel, those old photos document the property accurately — they just look dated in resolution.

**Archival property photographs.** Century-old farmhouses, Victorian homes, historic commercial buildings, and heritage properties often have remarkable archival photographs — construction photos, real estate listings from the 1950s, architect's documentation photos. These can be powerful marketing tools for buyers who love historic character, but they need restoration to be usable.

**Scanned print photos from pre-digital era.** Brokers and long-tenured agents sometimes have physical listing photos going back to the 1980s and early 1990s. These pre-digital photos were taken on film and printed, and they've been sitting in files for 30–40 years. A scanner can digitize them, but the resulting file needs enhancement.

## Where AI Enhancement Falls Short for Real Estate

**AI cannot change what a photo shows.** If the landscaping in a 2015 photo has been replaced, if there's now a deck that didn't exist, if the exterior paint color has changed — the photo shows the old state. No AI enhancement will update the photograph to show the current property condition. Using a materially different photo for an active listing misrepresents the property.

**AI cannot replace professional lighting.** A poorly composed, badly lit photo of an interior will be a slightly sharper poorly composed, badly lit photo after AI processing. Composition, perspective, and lighting are the three factors that determine a listing photo's marketing effectiveness. AI enhancement addresses technical quality issues (resolution, grain, sharpness) but not photographic craft.

**AI cannot create virtual staging.** Virtual staging — digitally inserting furniture into empty rooms — is a separate category of AI tool entirely. ArtImageHub is a restoration and enhancement tool; it does not perform virtual staging.

**AI cannot improve photos where the key information is missing.** A listing photo taken before a significant renovation doesn't document the renovation. The market needs current, accurate documentation of what buyers are purchasing.
