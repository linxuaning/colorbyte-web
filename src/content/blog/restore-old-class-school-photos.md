You inherit a parent's school yearbook from the 1960s, or a stack of class photos from your own school days. The pages are yellowed, faces are soft, and you want to restore the photos for family-history records or class-reunion display.

This guide walks through restoring old class and school photographs with attention to the unique challenges of group photos with many small faces.

## Why school photos challenge AI restoration

Unlike formal portraits (one face filling the frame), school photos have specific challenges:

- **Many small faces**: A class of 25 students means each face is ~3-5% of frame
- **Variable lighting**: Outdoor or classroom lighting often inconsistent
- **Group composition**: Subjects at different distances from camera = different focus
- **Caption text**: Often handwritten, faded, or printed in small type
- **Yearbook quality**: 1950s-1980s yearbooks used economical printing that loses detail

The closest, largest faces in a class photo restore best. Back-row and corner subjects see less improvement.

## Step 1: Sort your collection

**Yearbook portraits** (studio quality, restore well):
- Senior portraits, individual student photos
- Faculty portraits
- Awards/achievement portraits

**Class group photos** (variable, depends on framing):
- Annual class photos
- Sports team photos
- Club/activity group shots

**Informal photos** (challenging):
- Classroom candids
- Field trip photos
- Outdoor school events

Triage by significance and restoration potential.

## Step 2: Maximum-resolution scanning

For school photos especially, scan at high resolution:
- Yearbook portraits: 1200+ DPI
- Class group photos: **2400+ DPI** (gives AI more data per face)
- Yearbook page scans: 600 DPI for whole pages, then crop to individual photos at higher effective resolution

For yearbook digitization, scan whole pages first to preserve layout, then extract individual photos for AI restoration.

## Step 3: Batch process yearbook portraits

Yearbook portraits typically have:
- Consistent studio lighting
- Similar pose conventions
- Same era/print quality

These can be batch-processed:
1. Load all portraits into AI tool
2. Apply consistent restoration settings
3. Review batch output
4. Manual touchup on outliers

[ArtImageHub](/old-photo-restoration) supports batch upload for this workflow.

## Step 4: Selective restoration for class group photos

Class group photos need different approach:
1. Run AI restoration on whole image (auto-mode)
2. Review which faces are now sharp vs still soft
3. For unsharp central subjects, consider crop + individual face restoration
4. Reassemble crops into composite if needed for display

For genealogy archives, the whole-class restored photo is sufficient. For specific descendant research (find great-uncle in his 6th grade class photo), individual face crops help.

## Step 5: Caption and label restoration

Class photos often have:
- Handwritten student names below the photo
- Printed teacher names
- Year and class designation
- Sometimes school name in photo or on mount

These caption areas benefit from AI text-aware sharpening:
1. Crop caption area as separate image
2. Apply AI restoration with text-priority settings
3. Cross-reference restored names with online yearbook databases

## Step 6: Yearbook archive workflow

For digitizing entire yearbooks:
1. Scan each page at 600 DPI (preserve layout)
2. Extract individual photos at higher effective resolution
3. Batch AI restoration on individual extractions
4. Reassemble enhanced photos into yearbook PDF
5. Add OCR layer for searchable names

This produces a digital yearbook that's both visually restored and searchable.

## Special case: graduation photos

Graduation photos (cap, gown, diploma) have particular significance and often restore well:
- Studio quality (similar to yearbook portraits)
- Single subject focus
- Formal pose

For irreplaceable graduation photos (deceased relatives, immigrant family first-graduates), restoration is high-priority and rewards effort.

## Special case: foreign-language school photos

For school photos from non-English schools (immigrant ancestor's homeland school):
- Caption text may be in another language
- AI sharpening still works on text shape (helps native speakers read)
- Translation services + restored captions = full context

## Cross-reference resources

After restoration, identify subjects through:

**Free**:
- [FamilySearch yearbook collection](https://www.familysearch.org/) — free with account
- Local school district archives (often online)
- State historical society yearbook collections

**Subscription**:
- [Classmates.com](https://www.classmates.com/) — extensive yearbook database
- [Ancestry yearbook collection](https://www.ancestry.com/) — integrated with family tree

## Realistic expectations

Class photo restoration outcomes:

- **Yearbook portrait + light fade**: AI produces excellent results
- **Class group + moderate fade**: Strong improvement on closest faces, modest on background
- **Sports team or club photo**: Variable; depends on original composition
- **Severely faded outdoor photo**: Limited recovery; AI cannot create what isn't there
