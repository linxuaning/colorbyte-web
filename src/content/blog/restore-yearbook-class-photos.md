## Yearbook Photo Challenges

### Common Issues

**Printing Quality:**
- Yearbook printing often lower quality than photo prints
- Halftone dot patterns visible
- Ink density variations
- Paper quality affects preservation

**Photo Size:**
- Individual portraits often small
- Group photos have tiny faces
- Need significant enlargement

Our [AI photo upscaler](/ai-photo-upscaler) enlarges yearbook faces while adding natural detail.

### Scanning Yearbook Pages

**Best Practices:**
- Scan at 600-1200 DPI
- Keep book flat but don't crack spine
- Use book edge scanning mode if available
- Handle old yearbooks gently

## Types of School Photos

### Class Pictures

**Group Photo Challenges:**
- Many small faces
- Even lighting variations
- Paper texture visible
- Fading affects everyone

### Individual Portraits

**Portrait Issues:**
- Standard yearbook size is small
- Cropping limits enhancement options
- Background often plain gray

### Candid Shots

**Activity Photos:**
- Often lower quality printing
- Action and movement blur
- Varied lighting conditions

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can tiny yearbook faces be enlarged to portrait size?**

Short answer: Yes, AI upscaling can significantly enlarge small yearbook faces. Results depend on original print quality, but even small images often produce surprisingly good enlarged portraits. Our [upscaler](/ai-photo-upscaler) adds natural detail during enlargement.

**How do I reduce the dot pattern visible in yearbook prints?**

Short answer: Scan at high DPI and let AI restoration smooth the halftone pattern. Our [restoration tool](/enhance-old-photos) reduces printing artifacts while preserving facial detail. Some dot pattern may remain in heavily printed areas.

**Are yearbook photos copyrighted?**

Short answer: Personal use restoration of yearbook photos is generally fine. For commercial use or publication, the school or yearbook company may hold rights. Class reunions and memorial use typically don't raise concerns.

**What's the best way to restore photos for a class reunion?**

Short answer: Start with the main class group photo and key individual portraits. Scan at high DPI from original yearbooks. Our restoration tool can then enhance multiple photos quickly. Consider creating printed displays or digital slideshows.
