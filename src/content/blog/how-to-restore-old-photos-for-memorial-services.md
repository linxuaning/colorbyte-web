## What Resolution Do You Need for Poster-Sized Memorial Display?

The most visually prominent photo display at most memorial services is a large central portrait — typically 16x20 or 20x24 inches — displayed on an easel near the entry or near the casket or urn. This print is viewed at close range for extended periods, and resolution matters.

**For 16x20 inch output at 150 DPI:** You need 2400x3000 pixels minimum. This is the practical floor for a print displayed at the 3-to-5-foot viewing distances typical of an easel-mounted memorial portrait. At this resolution and viewing distance, most viewers will not notice pixelation unless they look very closely.

**For 16x20 inch output at 300 DPI:** You need 4800x6000 pixels. This is the professional standard for high-quality large-format printing. Most printing services that accept large-format orders can process files at this resolution.

**What this means for scanning:** A 4x6 photograph scanned at 600 DPI produces a 2400x3600 pixel file — sufficient for 16x20 output at 150 DPI without upscaling. **Real-ESRGAN** can upscale this source further, potentially reaching the 4800x6000 pixel range needed for 300 DPI output from a well-exposed original. For small originals (wallet size, 3x3 studio prints), scan at 1200 DPI to give Real-ESRGAN sufficient source material.

**Practical recommendation:** For a central memorial portrait to be printed at 16x20, scan the original at 1200 DPI if it is 4x6 or smaller. Upload to ArtImageHub. Preview the AI-restored result. Download the full-resolution file at $4.99. Take the TIFF or PNG download to FedEx Office or a local print lab for large-format output.

## Which Damage Types Does AI Resolve Quickly, and Which Require More Time?

The processing time at ArtImageHub is 20 to 35 seconds for most photographs regardless of damage type. The relevant distinction is not processing time but result quality — which damage types produce reliably good results, and which require preview evaluation before the $4.99 download decision.

### AI Resolves Reliably in One Pass

**Fading and color shift** in chromogenic prints (Kodacolor, Fujifilm, standard print labs) is consistently corrected by **DDColor** and Real-ESRGAN working together. The color balance correction from typical cyan-dye-fade orange shift or Fujifilm green shift is reliable for standard fading levels.

**Surface scratches** narrower than 2 to 3 millimeters are removed with high accuracy. Linear scratches that run across faces, backgrounds, and clothing areas disappear in the restoration without visible reconstruction artifacts in most cases.

**Film grain and analog noise** is distinguished from image content by **NAFNet** and separated cleanly, producing smoother skin tones and cleaner background areas without destroying genuine image texture.

**Soft focus from consumer cameras** — the slight softness characteristic of Kodak Instamatic, early Polaroid, and similar consumer camera photographs — is significantly improved by Real-ESRGAN edge recovery.

**Face recovery from studio portraits** using GFPGAN is reliable for portraits taken in professional or semi-professional studio conditions where the face is the primary subject, occupies a significant portion of the frame, and retains basic structural features despite fading or light damage.

### AI Produces Variable Results Requiring Preview Evaluation

**Black-and-white colorization** via DDColor is plausible but not guaranteed accurate for every subject. Evaluate the preview specifically for skin tones, clothing colors, and any objects where color accuracy matters for family recognition of the scene.

**Severely blurry candid photos** with faces that are very small in the frame (fewer than 50 pixels wide) show limited improvement from GFPGAN because the facial structure data is insufficient for the model to anchor its reconstruction. The photo may improve meaningfully in other areas while the face remains soft.

**Very underexposed photographs** with minimal tonal information — dark, murky images where shadow detail is almost entirely absent — have limited recoverable content for the AI pipeline. The result may be cleaner but remain too dark for useful display.

**Large areas of missing content** — a photograph with a torn corner, a section of image physically absent — shows AI reconstruction that is visible as generated content rather than authentic image information. For memorial use, decide in the preview whether the reconstruction is usable or whether the photo is better displayed as-is.
