> **Updated 2026-05-01**: AI model lineage clarified — most consumer photo restoration tools (including those compared here) wrap derivatives of **GFPGAN** ([arXiv:2101.04061](https://arxiv.org/abs/2101.04061), Tencent ARC Lab 2021) for face restoration and **Real-ESRGAN** ([arXiv:2107.10833](https://arxiv.org/abs/2107.10833), 2021) for upscaling. Differences between products are mostly pricing model and workflow, not raw AI quality.


The photo restoration market in 2026 spans AI-powered browser tools, mobile apps, and professional human services. Here's a ranked comparison for the most common use case: restoring old, faded, or damaged family photographs.

---

## Rankings: Best Photo Restoration Services 2026

### #1 ArtImageHub — Best for Old Family Photos

**Price:** $4.99/photo, one-time  
**Quality:** Excellent (CodeFormer + GFPGAN + Real-ESRGAN)  
**Speed:** 30–90 seconds  
**Ease:** Upload and download, no account required

**Why it ranks #1:** ArtImageHub runs the strongest available AI pipeline for historical photographic restoration. CodeFormer's face reconstruction was specifically trained on the type of degradation common in 1940s–1970s photographs. GFPGAN's systematic fading correction goes beyond general brightness adjustment. Real-ESRGAN handles upscaling cleanly.

The price-to-quality ratio is unmatched for one-time restoration projects. No subscription, no watermark, 30-day guarantee.

**Best for:** Portraits from any decade, significantly damaged prints, genealogy projects, gift projects.

---

### #2 MyHeritage Photo Enhancer — Best for Genealogy Integration

**Price:** Free (watermarked) / $149/year (Complete)  
**Quality:** Good  
**Speed:** Fast  
**Ease:** Account required; integrated with family tree

**Why it ranks #2:** MyHeritage's photo restoration quality is good — below ArtImageHub on heavily damaged historical portraits, but solid for moderate damage. The family tree integration is genuinely valuable for genealogy users — restored photos link directly to family profiles.

**Best for:** MyHeritage users, genealogy projects where tree integration matters.

---

### #3 Remini — Best Mobile App

**Price:** Free (limited) / $4.99–$9.99/month  
**Quality:** Good  
**Speed:** Fast  
**Ease:** iOS/Android app

**Why it ranks #3:** Remini's face enhancement quality is good, particularly for portraits. The mobile-first design makes it accessible without a computer. Monthly subscription may not be cost-effective for one-time projects.

**Best for:** Mobile users, portrait enhancement, regular processing rather than one-time projects.

---

### #4 Adobe Photoshop (Manual) — Best for Maximum Control

**Price:** $20.99/month (Creative Cloud)  
**Quality:** Excellent (with skill)  
**Speed:** 30–60 minutes per photo  
**Ease:** Significant learning curve

**Why it ranks #4:** Photoshop's restoration capability with a skilled operator — healing brush, clone stamp, curves, Neural Filters, frequency separation — produces the highest quality results on complex damage. The cost: learning Photoshop and investing significant time per photo.

**Best for:** Professional restoration, complex damage requiring manual artistic judgment.

---

### #5 RetouchUp — Best Professional Online Service

**Price:** $10–$50/photo  
**Quality:** Excellent (human professional)  
**Speed:** 2–7 days  
**Ease:** Upload and wait; no skill required

**Why it ranks #5:** RetouchUp connects you with professional photo restorers who work manually in Photoshop. Quality is high; price is higher than AI tools; turnaround is days rather than seconds.

**Best for:** Photos where AI results aren't satisfactory, historically significant single photos.

---

### #6 Fotor — Best Multi-Tool Platform with Restoration

**Price:** Free (watermarked) / $8.99–$19.99/month  
**Quality:** Good  
**Speed:** Fast  
**Ease:** Browser-based, easy interface

**Why it ranks #6:** Fotor's restoration quality is decent for moderate damage. The multi-tool platform (editing, design, generation, restoration) adds value if you use other features. Watermark-free download requires subscription.

**Best for:** Users who need a full editing + restoration platform.

---

### #7 GIMP — Best Free Option

**Price:** Free  
**Quality:** Excellent (with skill)  
**Speed:** 30–60+ minutes  
**Ease:** Significant learning curve

**Why it ranks #7:** GIMP is a full professional photo editor with manual restoration tools. The results can be excellent — but require learning the software and investing significant time. Free, open-source, local processing.

**Best for:** Technical users, budget-constrained projects, privacy-sensitive users.
