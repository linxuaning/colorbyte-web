# Restoring 1980s Reagan Era Family Photos: Morning in America, Fading in Albums

The 1980s produced a specific visual culture — bold colors, big hair, conspicuous consumption — that consumer photography documented with enthusiasm. The family photographs from this decade are both personal history and documents of a specific cultural moment.

## The 1980s Color Problem

1980s color photography shows two competing tendencies: the neon-saturated intentional colors of the era and the warm fading that consumer Kodacolor and Fujicolor films have experienced over 40 years. Restoring 1980s photographs means recovering the specific palette of the era — which was intentionally bold — while removing the inadvertent fading.

## Consumer Culture in Family Photos

1980s family photographs document the consumer culture of the Reagan era in specific detail: the specific clothing brands, the electronics that were status symbols, the cars, the home decor. This material culture, visible in the background of family photographs, has increasing historical and sociological value.

## The Decade of the Portrait Studio

The 1980s saw a resurgence of professional portrait photography for consumer families — Sears portrait studios, JCPenney portraits, and mall portrait studios brought professional portraiture to a mass market. These photographs, produced at scale, show the decade's aesthetic conventions in concentrated form.
