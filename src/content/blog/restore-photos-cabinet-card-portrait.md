Cabinet cards were the social media of Victorian America — formal portraits printed on thick cardboard cards that were exchanged, collected, and displayed on cabinet shelves. These images document how 19th-century Americans presented themselves to the world.

## The Challenge

Cabinet card photos used albumen printing processes that deteriorated in predictable patterns — overall yellowing, fading in highlight areas, and sometimes the crystallization of the albumen surface. Many also show physical damage from a century of handling and storage.

## How AI Helps

AI restoration applies albumen print-specific algorithms that reverse the yellowing and fading patterns of Victorian cabinet cards. The restoration recovers the original warm tonal range of albumen prints, distinguishing photographic tonality from deterioration yellowing.
