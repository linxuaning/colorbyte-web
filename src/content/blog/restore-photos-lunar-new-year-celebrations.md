Lunar New Year celebrations produce a specific category of family photographs: the formal family gatherings, the specific decorations, the traditional clothing that may only appear in photographs from this occasion.

## Cultural Documentation in Holiday Photos

Lunar New Year photographs document specific cultural practices: the specific foods, the decorations, the red envelopes, the family gathering configurations. These details, recovered through restoration, document cultural heritage that may be less visible in everyday family photography.

## Multiple Generation Documentation

Lunar New Year photographs often capture multiple generations gathered together — a family event that reliably brings the full extended family into the same space. These multi-generational photographs are among the most valuable for family history.

## Traditional Clothing Archive

The traditional clothing worn for Lunar New Year celebrations — which may appear in family photographs only once a year — creates a specific archive of traditional dress that documents cultural connection across generations.

## Getting the Best Results

Start with the highest-quality scan you can produce — 600 DPI minimum for standard prints, 1200 DPI for small prints or photographs with faces you want to identify. Color mode scanning, even for black-and-white photographs, gives AI restoration algorithms more information to work with.

After restoration, compare the result with the original at full zoom. Check faces carefully to ensure identity is preserved, and note any areas where AI may have filled in damaged sections with plausible but uncertain reconstructions.
