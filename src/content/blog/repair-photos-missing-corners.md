## Types of Corner and Edge Damage

Understanding the damage type helps choose the most effective restoration approach.

### Common Damage Patterns

**Missing Corners**:
- Single corner torn or cut off
- Multiple corners affected
- Diagonal tears from corner
- Crumbling from age
- Dog-eared and lost pieces

**Torn Edges**:
- Straight tears along edge
- Irregular rip patterns
- Multiple small tears
- Frayed or ragged edges
- Triangular chunks missing

**Rounded or Worn Edges**:
- Corners softened from handling
- Edges worn thin
- Surface abrasion along borders
- Photo physically smaller than original
- Often uniform around perimeter

**Album Damage**:
- Corners torn during removal
- Adhesive-related edge loss
- Photo corner mount damage
- Backing paper remains attached
- Multiple overlapping damage types

## Physical Restoration Methods

For those with original photo pieces or wanting physical repair before digital restoration.

### If You Have the Missing Pieces

**Alignment and Attachment**:

1. **Clean both pieces gently**
2. **Align carefully on light box or bright surface**
3. **Use archival photo tape** on back only
4. **Never use regular tape** (damages over time)
5. **Consider professional mounting** for valuable photos

**Archival Repair Materials**:
- Filmoplast tape (reversible)
- Japanese tissue and wheat paste
- Archival document repair tape
- Photo-safe adhesives
- Never use regular adhesives

### Professional Conservation

For valuable photographs, consider professional treatment:

**What Conservators Offer**:
- Inpainting (manual reconstruction)
- Paper stabilization
- Professional mounting and matting
- Archival backing
- Controlled humidity flattening

**When to Seek Professional Help**:
- Historically significant photographs
- Severe structural damage
- Photos requiring display
- Insurance or legal needs
- Irreplaceable family heirlooms

### Scanning Damaged Photos

For best AI restoration results:

**Scanning Tips**:
- Include entire photo, even damaged edges
- Use scanner bed, not glass cover on edges
- Scan at 600 DPI minimum
- Keep photo flat but don't force
- Include any loose pieces in scan

**Documentation**:
- Note which areas are original
- Keep unedited scan archived
- Document restoration extent
- Consider marking restored versions

## Prevention and Care

Protect your photographs from future corner and edge damage.

### Handling Best Practices

**Always**:
- Hold by edges only
- Use cotton gloves for valuable photos
- Support entire photo when moving
- Use both hands for larger prints

**Never**:
- Touch emulsion surface
- Bend or fold photos
- Stack without interleaving
- Force photos into tight spaces

### Storage Protection

**Recommended**:
- Individual acid-free sleeves
- Archival albums with corner mounts
- Flat storage in proper boxes
- Climate-controlled environment

**Avoid**:
- Rubber bands around photos
- Paperclips or staples
- Overstuffed albums
- Contact with acidic materials
