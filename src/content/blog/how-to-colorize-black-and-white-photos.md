## How AI Colorization Works

AI colorization is genuinely different from what it sounds like. It's not simply 'painting' color onto a black and white image — it's applying a neural network trained on millions of color photographs to make informed guesses about the likely colors in a scene. The AI recognizes faces, clothing textures, sky, grass, wood, skin tones, and hundreds of other common elements, and assigns them the colors they most likely would have been in real life. The results are remarkable for scenes where colors are predictable (blue sky, green grass, brown wood), and thoughtful for scenes where colors are ambiguous.

## Realistic Expectations: What AI Can and Cannot Know

There are limits to what any AI can know about color from a black and white image alone. It can't know that your great-grandmother's dress was her favorite deep burgundy rather than blue, or that the wallpaper behind a family portrait was a specific shade of sage green. For mass-produced items (grass, sky, typical 1940s cars in their common colors), AI colorization is usually accurate. For personal items like clothing and home interiors, it makes educated guesses based on what was commonly used in the period. If you have family members who remember the original colors of specific items, note those and look for tools that allow manual color correction.

## Combining Colorization with Restoration

The best results come from restoring a black and white photo first (removing damage, improving sharpness and contrast) and then colorizing the clean, restored image. Starting with a damaged, scratched image often causes colorization AI to misread damaged areas as objects and apply incorrect colors.
