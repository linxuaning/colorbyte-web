## Understanding Motion Blur

Motion blur occurs when something moves during the camera's exposure time, creating a streaky or smeared effect rather than a sharp image.

### Types of Motion Blur

**Camera Shake**:
- Entire image affected
- Uniform blur direction
- Handheld long exposures
- Unstable camera support

**Subject Motion**:
- Moving person/object blurred
- Background often sharp
- Partial image affected
- Common with children, pets, dancers

**Combined Motion**:
- Both camera and subject moved
- Complex blur patterns
- Most challenging to address
- Common in candid shots

### Why Motion Blur Was Common

| Era | Cause | Prevalence |
|-----|-------|-----------|
| Pre-1900 | Very slow film, long exposures | Very common |
| 1900-1950 | Slower films, basic cameras | Common |
| 1950-1980 | Improved but still slow film | Occasional |
| Post-1980 | Faster films, auto exposure | Less common |

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can AI completely remove motion blur from old photos?**

Short answer: AI can significantly reduce motion blur effects, but complete removal isn't physically possible—information lost to blur can't be perfectly recovered. Our [AI restoration tool](/enhance-old-photos) substantially improves clarity, making faces recognizable and details visible. Expect meaningful improvement, not perfect sharpness.

**Why were old photos more prone to motion blur?**

Short answer: Film sensitivity was much lower in earlier eras, requiring longer exposure times. Even small movements during these extended exposures caused blur. Early photographers posed subjects with head braces, yet slight movement still occurred. Faster films and better cameras reduced but didn't eliminate the problem.

**Can motion blur caused by children or pets be fixed?**

Short answer: Yes, partial blur where the subject moved but background is sharp is a good restoration candidate. Our AI processes the blurred subject area while preserving sharp elements. Results depend on blur severity—slight movement blur improves dramatically, severe blur improves moderately.

**How do I know if my blurred photo can be improved?**

Short answer: If you can make out facial features and basic shapes despite the blur, improvement is likely. Try our [free restoration tool](/restore-old-family-photos)—processing is quick and results speak for themselves. Even moderately blurred photos often show surprising improvement.

**Is motion blur different from focus blur for restoration purposes?**

Short answer: Yes, they're technically different problems. Motion blur has directional characteristics (streaking), while focus blur is more uniform softness. Modern AI handles both, but treats them differently. Motion blur often responds well to directional correction; focus blur benefits from overall sharpening and detail enhancement.
