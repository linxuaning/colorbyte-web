Korean War era photos document the families of 1.8 million Americans who served in 'the forgotten war' — the departure ceremonies, the waiting, the homecomings, and the losses that this conflict imposed on families across America.

## The Challenge

Early 1950s photography captured the transition to color consumer photography. Many Korean War era family collections mix black-and-white prints with early color prints, creating preservation challenges across multiple technologies.

## How AI Helps

AI restoration handles mixed-technology collections from the early 1950s color transition period, applying era-appropriate treatment to each image type while normalizing the visual quality of the complete family archive.

## Next Steps

Gather your photographs, scan at the highest resolution available (600 DPI minimum), and visit [PhotoFix](/old-photo-restoration) to start the restoration process. AI tools can handle damage that once required expensive professional services, making preservation accessible for every family.
