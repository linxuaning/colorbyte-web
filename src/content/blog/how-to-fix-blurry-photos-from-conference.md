## Why Do Conference Photos Turn Out Blurry in the First Place?

Stage lighting is the primary culprit. Conference lighting rigs are designed to make speakers look good to a live audience, not to provide the bright, even illumination photographers need. This forces cameras into high-ISO, slow-shutter compromises that introduce noise and motion blur simultaneously.

Add long focal lengths in wide auditoriums, JPEG compression from smartphone cameras, and subjects moving unpredictably between slides, and you have a perfect recipe for blurry images. The good news: these are exactly the degradation types that modern AI models like NAFNet and Real-ESRGAN were trained to reverse.
