## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can Korean War combat photos be restored?**

Short answer: Usually yes, significantly. Combat photos often suffered from field conditions, but our [AI restoration tool](/enhance-old-photos) can recover detail, correct fading, and repair damage. Results depend on original condition, but meaningful improvement is typically possible.

**How do I identify my relative's unit from Korean War photos?**

Short answer: Look for shoulder patches and insignia. Research Army divisions, Marine regiments, and other units that served. The National Archives has Korean War unit records. Unit association websites often help identify specific patches and markings.

**Were the 1973 fire records destroyed for Korean War veterans?**

Short answer: The 1973 National Archives fire primarily affected Army records for those discharged 1912-1964, so many Korean War Army veterans' records were affected. However, alternative sources exist: unit records, VA files, casualty records, and state archives. Navy, Marine, and Air Force records were less affected.

**Can Korean War color photos be restored?**

Short answer: Yes, often dramatically. Early Kodachrome can be well-preserved or significantly faded. Our restoration tool handles both scenarios—enhancing well-preserved color or correcting severe color shifts.

**How do I honor a Korean War veteran through their photographs?**

Short answer: Restore images using our [free tool](/restore-old-family-photos), research their service, document their story, and consider sharing copies with the Korean War Veterans Memorial education center. Create family archives that connect photos to service records and personal memories.
