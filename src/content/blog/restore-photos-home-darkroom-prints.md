Home darkroom photography represented the most engaged relationship a person could have with the photographic medium — mixing chemicals, exposing paper under the enlarger red light, watching images emerge in developer trays. These prints carry the maker's direct creative involvement.

## The Challenge

Home darkroom prints show quality variation that reflects the photographer's experience and the chemistry they used. Some home darkroom photographers produced archival-quality prints; others used improper fixing or washing that created chemically unstable prints.

## How AI Helps

AI restoration identifies the specific deterioration patterns of improperly processed home darkroom prints — sulfur staining from inadequate fixing, silver mirroring from poor washing — and applies targeted chemical reversal algorithms that recover the photographer's original vision.

## Next Steps
