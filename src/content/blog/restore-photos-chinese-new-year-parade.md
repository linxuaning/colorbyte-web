# Restoring Chinese New Year Parade Photographs

Chinese New Year parade photographs document one of the most visually spectacular annual celebrations in American Chinese communities: the dragon dances, the lion dances, the firecrackers, and the crowded streets of Chinatowns transformed into festival spaces. For Chinese American families with connections to these community celebrations, photographs from New Year parades document both personal participation and the community institution that has maintained Chinese cultural traditions in American cities for over a century.

## Cultural Heritage

Chinese New Year parade photographs have a specific visual character reflecting both the festivity of the celebration and the photography conditions of outdoor public events: the bright colors of dragon and lion costumes, the crowds of spectators, the firecracker smoke that gives many photographs a distinctive atmospheric quality, and the specific settings of Chinatown streets decorated for the holiday. For families involved in parade organizations or lion dance troupes, photographs document both performance and organizational community.

## Parade Documentation

Chinese New Year photographs face preservation challenges related to their outdoor festival contexts and to the specific conditions of Chinese American household storage across different eras of Chinese American life.
