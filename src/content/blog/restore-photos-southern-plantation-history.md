# Restoring Photographs From the American South's Complex History

Photographs from the antebellum and post-Civil War American South document one of American history's most complex periods. These photographs show both the lives of white Southern families in their specific regional cultural context and, often in the background or explicitly in the foreground, the enslaved and later sharecropping Black families whose labor underpinned Southern society. Restoring these photographs requires acknowledging this complexity.

## Historical Complexity

Southern plantation-era photographs have a specific visual character: the elaborate formal portraits of white planter-class families showing their wealth through clothing and setting, the informal documentation of plantation operations that sometimes included enslaved people in service roles, and the formal portraits of free Black families in Southern cities who maintained lives of dignity and achievement despite the conditions of the era.
