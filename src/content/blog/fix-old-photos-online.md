## How to Fix Old Photos Online: Step-by-Step

### Step 1: Scan or photograph your original

The quality of your starting image determines the ceiling of your result. For best results:

- Use a flatbed scanner at 600 DPI minimum (1200 DPI for small originals)
- If scanning isn't an option, photograph in indirect natural light, flat on a surface, directly overhead
- Avoid flash—it creates glare on glossy photo paper
- JPEG is fine; TIFF is better if your scanner supports it

### AI handles these reliably:

| Damage Type | AI Performance | Notes |
|-------------|---------------|-------|
| Color fading | Excellent | Restores saturation and contrast based on surviving color data |
| Scratches and fine lines | Very good | Detects and removes linear damage artifacts |

| Water stains | Good | Reduces discoloration; severe cases may require manual review |
| Torn edges | Good | Fills missing edge areas using context from the rest of the image |

### AI has limits with:

- **Large missing sections**: If more than 20% of the image is gone, AI fills with plausible but invented content
- **Extreme blur**: If the original capture was severely out of focus, AI can sharpen slightly but can't recover lost detail
- **Ink or pen marks over faces**: Text or markings directly on subjects are difficult to remove cleanly
- **Multiple overlapping damage types**: Very complex damage benefits from human professional review

## Getting the Best Results When You Fix Old Photos Online

1. **Clean your scanner glass** before scanning—dust on the glass creates artifacts that AI may interpret as photo damage
2. **Scan at higher resolution than you think you need**—you can always downsize, but you can't upsize without losing quality
3. **Don't pre-process your photo** before uploading—let the AI see the original damage; pre-sharpening or contrast adjustments can confuse the restoration algorithm
4. **Keep the original scan** before downloading the restoration—your original is irreplaceable; the AI result is replaceable
5. **Compare carefully** before finalizing—use the side-by-side comparison to verify the AI preserved all the important details and didn't invent content in damaged areas
