# Preserving Disability History and Heritage Photos

Disability community photos document the history of the disability rights movement — the Capitol Crawl ADA demonstration, Independent Living Center communities, and the daily lives of disabled people who fought for the right to full participation in American society.

## The Challenge

Disability community archive photos were often stored in organizational offices that were not designed as archival spaces. The rolling environmental conditions of community organization offices created varied deterioration patterns across collections.

## How AI Helps

AI restoration normalizes the technical quality of disability community archive photos, making these historically important documents accessible and legible for researchers, activists, and community members documenting the history of the disability rights movement.

## Next Steps

Gather your photographs, scan at the highest resolution available (600 DPI minimum), and visit [fix old photos free](/old-photo-restoration) to start the restoration process. AI tools can handle damage that once required expensive professional services, making preservation accessible for every family.
