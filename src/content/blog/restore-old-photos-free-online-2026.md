## What "free" really means in 2026

The "free online photo restoration" landscape splits into 4 categories:

### 1. Truly free (open-source local)

- **[Upscayl](https://upscayl.org)** — desktop GUI for Real-ESRGAN, one-click installer for Mac/Windows/Linux
- **[GFPGAN](https://github.com/TencentARC/GFPGAN)** — face restoration model from Tencent ARC Lab, requires Python install
- **[Real-ESRGAN](https://github.com/xinntao/Real-ESRGAN)** — image upscaling model

What you get:
- Unwatermarked output ✓
- High resolution (no caps) ✓
- Unlimited usage ✓
- Privacy (runs locally, no upload) ✓

What it costs:
- 30+ minutes initial setup
- Requires modern computer (recent Mac M1+/M2 or Windows with discrete GPU recommended)
- No customer support
- Some technical literacy needed

For technical users, this is the BEST FREE option. For non-technical users, the friction makes it not viable.

### 2. Watermarked free web tier

- **DeepAI** ([deepai.org](https://deepai.org)) free tier — limited free generations, watermarked
- **Restore.photos** free preview — small/limited generations, paywall for HD
- **Pixlr** AI tools — free with limitations

What you get:
- Free with no install ✓
- Browser-based (any device) ✓

What you DON'T get:
- Watermark across output ✗
- Resolution typically capped at 1024px ✗
- Limited generations (3-10/day typical) ✗

Useful for: One-off curiosity test, "is AI photo restoration any good for my photos?" sanity check.

NOT useful for: Archive purposes (watermarked), printing (resolution capped), batch projects (limited generations).

### 3. Free trial / preview

- **Remini free trial** — limited free generations on first install of mobile app, then paywall
- **MyHeritage free preview** — generates a small/blurred preview, full version requires subscription

These are PAID services with a free preview to drive subscription conversion. Useful for trying before subscribing.

## Why "free" AI quality is similar to "paid"

Most consumer photo restoration tools wrap derivatives of the same open-source AI models:

- **Face restoration**: [GFPGAN](https://arxiv.org/abs/2101.04061) (Wang et al., Tencent ARC Lab 2021) is dominant, used by Remini, MyHeritage, ArtImageHub, restore.photos, and most others.
- **Upscaling**: [Real-ESRGAN](https://arxiv.org/abs/2107.10833) (Wang et al. 2021) is dominant.

This means "AI quality" between free local install (Upscayl) and paid services (Remini, ArtImageHub, etc.) is largely similar. What you pay for in commercial tools:
- Removed watermark
- Higher resolution caps
- Batch features
- Polished UX
- Customer support
- Convenience (no install)

NOT better AI quality — that's largely marketing.
