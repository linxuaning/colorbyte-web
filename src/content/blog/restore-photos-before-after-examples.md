Setting realistic expectations for photo restoration is essential — some damage can be completely reversed, some can be significantly improved, and some results in permanent information loss. Understanding the spectrum of possible outcomes helps families prioritize their restoration projects.

## The Challenge

Completely reversible damage includes fading from light exposure, color shift from dye instability, surface scratches that don't penetrate the emulsion, and dust contamination. These issues reliably produce excellent restoration results in most cases.

## How AI Helps

Permanently lost information includes areas where fire destroyed the emulsion completely, photos that spent years fully submerged in contaminated water, and damage where the photographic layer has completely separated from the substrate. In these cases, restoration reconstructs plausible content — which may be useful but cannot be verified as historically accurate.

## Next Steps

Gather your photographs, scan at the highest resolution available (600 DPI minimum), and visit [PhotoFix](/old-photo-restoration) to start the restoration process. AI tools can handle damage that once required expensive professional services, making preservation accessible for every family.
