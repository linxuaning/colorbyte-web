# Restoring 1890s Bicycle Craze Photos: The First American Fitness Boom

The 1890s bicycle craze was one of the most significant cultural phenomena of the decade. For the first time, ordinary people had personal mechanical transportation — and a recreation that crossed class lines, gender lines, and geographic lines.

## The Bicycle as Social Revolution

The safety bicycle (as opposed to the penny-farthing) democratized cycling in the 1890s. Women, specifically, found in bicycling a freedom of movement that previous decades had denied them. The photographs of women cycling in the 1890s document a specific moment of social change as well as recreational activity.

## 1890s Photographic Technology

Bicycle photographs from the 1890s were typically albumen or early silver gelatin prints. The outdoor conditions and active subjects presented challenges that the photography of the era handled with varying success. Formal posed cycling photographs are most common; action shots are rare and technically challenging.

## Equipment as Historical Artifact

The specific bicycle models of the 1890s — the safety bicycle designs, the specific components, the clothing worn for cycling — are historically significant artifacts. Restoration that recovers equipment detail documents the material culture of the sport at its most culturally significant moment.
