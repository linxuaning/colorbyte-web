## Why Do Phone Photos Fail Handmade Product Listings?

### What Specific Problems Do Craft Seller Photos Have?

Phone cameras are optimized for people in natural light at arm's length. The moment you move close to a small object — a hand-stamped greeting card, a ceramic bead, a miniature painting — the camera's autofocus struggles, the depth of field collapses, and you get a softness that hides the very details that justify your pricing.

Add typical indoor lighting — overhead LEDs, table lamps, window light mixing with artificial — and you compound the problem with color casts and uneven exposure. The resulting photo looks amateurish not because the craft is low quality, but because the camera and environment were not matched to the subject.

The good news: these are exactly the problems AI photo enhancement was built to solve.

## How Does AI Sharpening Help Craft Product Photos?

### Which AI Models Recover Texture and Fine Detail?

[Real-ESRGAN](https://arxiv.org/abs/2107.10833) is a super-resolution model that reconstructs fine texture detail from blurry or low-resolution source images. For craft photography, this means the weave structure of a handwoven scarf, the brush texture of a painted ceramic surface, or the hammered finish of a metal cuff bracelet all become visible and tactile-feeling in the restored image — even if the original photo was too soft to show them clearly.

[SwinIR](https://arxiv.org/abs/2108.10257) adds edge sharpening and artifact removal on top of the upscaled base, giving crisp outlines to buttons, stitching, and inlay details that buyers need to see to feel confident in a purchase.

[NAFNet](https://arxiv.org/abs/2204.04675) handles denoising — removing the grainy, speckled look that phone photos develop in dim conditions — producing a clean, smooth background that makes your product pop rather than getting lost in visual noise.
