A photo book of restored family photographs is one of the most meaningful family keepsakes you can create — particularly for significant occasions (anniversaries, reunions, memorial gatherings) or as a gift for older family members. Here's how to do it well.

---

## Step 1: Gather and Select the Photos

**Start with the most meaningful photos, not every photo:**
- Portraits of ancestors (grandparents, great-grandparents)
- Family milestone photos (weddings, graduations, military service)
- Family group photos spanning different eras
- Rare or unique photos — the only known image of a specific person or event

A 20–30 photo book is more impactful than a 100-photo book where the selection hasn't been curated. The best family photo books tell a story; selecting the right photos is the editorial work.

**Inventory what exists:** Ask family members to look for old prints. Check the back of frames. Look through storage boxes. Libraries and genealogical societies sometimes have historical photos of local families.

---

## Step 2: Digitize

**Scan at 600 DPI minimum.** Photo book printing reveals quality differences — a low-resolution scan looks soft or pixelated at 5×7 or larger in print. Libraries have free flatbed scanners. Scanning services (ScanMyPhotos, Legacybox) handle large batches.

**Phone scanning:** Google PhotoScan for reflective prints; good indirect lighting for matte prints.

## Step 5: Design the Book

**Chronological organization** works well for family history books — it creates a narrative arc.

**Theme organization** works for milestone-specific books (a grandparent's life, a specific family era, a wedding lineage).

**Layout tips:**
- Let important portraits breathe — give them a full page or half page
- Group photos work well at smaller sizes in multi-photo layouts
- Mix sizes: some full-page, some half-page, some grid layouts
- Add captions with names, dates, and relationships — this transforms a photo collection into a family record

**Text additions:**
- Brief family context: "John and Mary Walsh, married 1922, Portland Oregon"
- Stories: if you know the story behind a photo, include it as a caption or sidebar
- Family tree context: where this person fits in the family lineage

## For Giving as a Gift

Order multiple copies when giving as a gift — most services reduce per-copy price for duplicates. A photo book that goes to 3 family members (grandparents, parents, one sibling) often costs less per copy than ordering individually.

Order well before the occasion: standard shipping takes 7–14 days, rush shipping 2–5 days. Most print services need 3–7 days to produce the book before shipping.
